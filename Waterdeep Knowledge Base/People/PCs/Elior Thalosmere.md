- Male, age 26, Medium, approximately 5 ft. 9 in., 160 lb.

- Born into House Thalosmere, a minor Waterdeep noble family described as precise and overlooked.
- House Thalosmere keeps records of a marginal or half-erased divine entity associated with correction, routing misassigned souls, and resolving divine error.
- Elior was born apparently human; an exact scar manifested down his spine during a childhood fever.
- The scar warms when something is wrong: divine magic misfires, fate contradicts itself, or a soul lingers where it should not.
- Elior does not view himself as chosen. He views himself as an unresolved exception and a function that has become active again.

## Background and Arc Notes

Elior is at the Yawning Portal as field research for House Thalosmere, not by accident. His family monitors irregularities: strange magic, displaced prophecy, inconsistent souls, and political incidents that resolve too cleanly. His spinal scar is a diagnostic instrument as much as a divine mark.

Around the party, Elior's scar reacts in distinct ways: Jonathan reads as properly filed divine power; Aladeen registers as someone who redirects consequences into performance; Davy creates sustained warmth as if mispositioned; Threestrings and Bonnie register as controlled interference and stable identity divergence; Broethuul feels like an incompatible fey filing system rather than an error.

House Thalosmere's private archive treats [[Threads/Crommor Incident|the Crommor Incident]] as misassigned blame, interrupted transmission, and suspected infernal interference. The archive suggests the ceremonial bugle was a transmission vector and that a correction preserved stability at the cost of truth. Elior's own annotation says the ledger does not balance.
