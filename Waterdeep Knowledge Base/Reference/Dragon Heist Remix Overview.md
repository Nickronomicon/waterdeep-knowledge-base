# Dragon Heist Remix Overview

DM-facing structural overview of the Alexandrian Remix as the campaign's primary adventure framework. This page is a runtime map, not a prose summary of the PDF.

## Core Premise

The Remix reframes *Dragon Heist* around a multi-faction [[Threads/Great Game]] for Neverember's Enigma: the lost Vault of Dragons, the [[Objects/Stone of Golorr]], and the Stone's missing Eyes.

The current major players are:

| Player | Public / Table Entry | Structural Role |
|---|---|---|
| [[Factions/Xanathar Guild]] | Street war, sewers, gazers, monsters, harbor salvage | Originally held the Stone and one Eye; responds violently and obsessively. |
| [[Factions/Zhentarim]] | Renaer/Floon kidnapping, flying-snake actors, Davil/Yagra split | Manshoonian faction holds or seeks an Eye; Doom Raider material may be separate at the table. |
| [[Factions/Cassalanters]] | Victoro loan, noble patronage, Davy/Crommor danger | Holds one Eye and wants the vault money for the Asmodean bargain. |
| [[Factions/Bregan D'aerthe]] / Jarlaxle | Zardoz Zord, Sea Maidens ships, nimblewright sales | Starts outside the Game but can enter through nimblewright records or ship surveillance. |
| Gralhunds | Noble actors not yet fully table-active | Out-of-depth middle faction around the Fireball/Nimblewright pivot. |

## Runtime Structure

1. Finding Floon introduces the Zhentarim/Xanathar conflict and Renaer's hidden Eye.
2. Trollskull gives the party a base, faction ties, local obligations, and time for missions.
3. Fireball/Nimblewright investigation reveals Dalakhar, the Stone, and Gralhund Villa.
4. After Gralhund Villa, the party either has the Stone or knows who took it.
5. The main loop begins: investigate factions, hit [[Reference/Remix Faction Outposts]], follow clues to lairs, and perform [[Reference/Remix Eye Heists]].
6. Once the Stone is restored, the campaign points to Brandath Crypts and the Vault.
7. [[Reference/Remix Response Teams]] and player-facing factions bring pressure to the party whenever they make noise, stall, or anger a player in the Game.

## Simple Operating Checklist

- If the party looks for an Eye or a Golorr lead, point them at a faction outpost.
- If the party angers or exposes a faction, deploy a response team.
- If the party flounders, use a response team, faction ally, or outpost clue to put a live lead in front of them.
- If a future prep note is too sprawling, return to this loop: outpost -> lair -> Eye -> consequences.

## Campaign Alignment

- The campaign is currently paused on the morning of Ches 16, before Trollskull's expected opening window.
- The Remix's Fireball and heavy Grand Game machinery have not fully triggered at the table yet.
- The party already has several early Remix-adjacent clues: [[Objects/Renaer's Locket]], [[Objects/Scrap of Note]], Maxeene's rock gnome lead, the Cassalanter loan, Zardoz exposure, and Broethuul's [[Objects/The Amberleaf Dispatch]].
- Use transcript truth first. Remix material is future-facing structure until it appears in play.
