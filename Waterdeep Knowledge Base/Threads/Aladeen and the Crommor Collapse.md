# Aladeen and the Crommor Collapse

[[People/PCs/Aladeen]] turned Anastasia Crommor's public fall into a famous performance bit. It brought him short-lived fame and helped Waterdeep remember the incident as comedy rather than possible conspiracy.

## Current Tension

Aladeen believes satire can make danger survivable and hold power accountable. The Crommor Collapse tests that belief because the joke may have protected the powerful and wounded the truthful.

He does not yet appear to know that [[People/PCs/Davy]] is Anastasia Crommor.

## Prep Hooks

- Aladeen learns Davy's former identity privately after a performance, pamphlet, noble remark, or Dorgar clue.
- A noble asks Aladeen to perform the Crommor Collapse in front of Davy.
- Aladeen chooses to weaponize his fame to make the city hear a corrected story.
- House Crommor or Cassalanter agents treat Aladeen as a liability.

## Open Questions

- Would Aladeen apologize, deflect, make amends through performance, or double down?
- Can Aladeen make Waterdeep stop laughing long enough to listen?
- Was his original satire ever manipulated by noble interests?
