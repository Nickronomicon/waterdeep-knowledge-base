# Crommor Incident

The Crommor Incident is the shared backstory knot around Anastasia Crommor's fall during Laeral Silverhand's ascension ceremony in 1489 DR. Publicly, it is remembered as humiliation and comedy. Privately, multiple PC-facing sources suggest misassigned blame, infernal interference, and a message that failed to reach Laeral.

## Public Version

House Crommor was honored with presenting a ceremonial bugle to Laeral Silverhand. Anastasia Crommor carried it forward, fell, broke the bugle, and loudly accused Victoro Cassalanter of tripping her. Cassalanter denied it. No formal charges followed. The scandal damaged House Crommor and became cultural shorthand for an overly public accusation.

Aladeen's Crommor Collapse routine turned the event into a citywide joke.

## Private Angles

- [[People/PCs/Davy]] is secretly Anastasia Crommor.
- [[People/PCs/Aladeen]] witnessed the incident and later popularized the joke.
- [[People/PCs/Jonathan Usman Sonny Theodore Icarus Charles Eagleshield]] had an unfinished private noble tie to Anastasia before House Crommor fell.
- [[People/PCs/Elior Thalosmere]] has access to House Thalosmere records classifying the event as misassigned blame, interrupted transmission, and suspected infernal interference.
- [[People/PCs/Broethuul]] sensed something binding and contractual around the event, similar to the strange fate-currents near his own arrival in Waterdeep.

## Working Secret

The bugle may have been a transmission vector for a warning meant for Laeral Silverhand. Davy's DM arc notes suggest the warning concerned Cassalanter infernal dealings and never reached Laeral.

This should remain a flexible DM truth until session play locks it down.

## Open Questions

- Did Victoro Cassalanter physically trip Anastasia, redirect the event, or react to another actor's move?
- Who created or placed the warning in the bugle?
- Was House Crommor already vulnerable, or did the incident become the permission structure for its fall?
- Does Laeral know a warning existed?
- Who currently benefits from the public memory remaining a joke?
