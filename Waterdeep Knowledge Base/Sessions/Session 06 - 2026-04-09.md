# Session 06 - 2026-04-09

Session 6 stayed in the hours between the grell fight and the opera on Ches 3, 1492 DR. The party returned to [[Places/Trollskull Manor]], dealt with worried neighbors, learned more of the manor's haunted history, received a strange Blackstaff report-and-reward exchange, checked in with local businesses, borrowed monster books, and prepared for the opera. The session ended with the party leaving for [[Places/Lightsinger Theater]]; the opera, [[People/NPCs/Mirt]], Harper pitch, and theater-spy material did not occur yet.

## Start State

- The party has just fled the [[Places/Fish Street Warehouse]] before the Watch could question them.
- [[People/NPCs/Armet]] survived; the grell and two handlers are dead.
- The party has the [[Objects/Dockside Transfer Receipt]] and [[Objects/Loose It Then Leave Note Fragment]].
- [[Objects/Ironbound Grell Crate|The ironbound grell crate]] was left behind at the warehouse.
- The opera appointment with [[People/NPCs/Mirt]] is later that evening.

## Major Scenes

### Return to Trollskull Alley

The party returned to Trollskull Alley visibly covered in grell remains and the residue of a dangerous Dock Ward morning. Elior split off on the way back, saying he needed to speak with his father after meeting [[People/NPCs/Vajra Safahr]], and would rejoin the party at Trollskull Manor when able.

The alley already knew something had happened before the party arrived. Curtains moved, doors opened and shut, and [[People/NPCs/Fala Lefaliir]] emerged from [[Places/Corellon's Crown]] to check on them. Fala noticed that the blood and posture were not ordinary, and that Elior was missing. They offered water and identified the residue as aberrant rather than beast or human blood.

[[People/NPCs/Tally Fellbranch]] of [[Places/The Bent Nail]] also came over. He brought drink and asked, not out of gossip, whether anything had followed the party back, because he would rather know before it took an interest in his shutters.

### The Urchins Demand the Story

[[People/NPCs/Nat]], [[People/NPCs/Jenks]], and [[People/NPCs/Squiddly]] burst out of Trollskull Manor with wild, mostly wrong stories about dragons, mercenary captains, giants, mind flayers, and the Dock Ward danger. Aladeen turned the grell incident into a quick performance about a floating monster, a clever trick, and dirty boots coming home to Trollskull Manor. The children loved it and began repeating the song while play-fighting outside.

### Lif and Trollskull History

Inside the taproom, the ghost had not repaired anything, but had kept the bar orderly:

- the old mugs were clean;
- cleaning supplies were put away;
- broken glass from earlier bottle activity was swept up;
- the bar top was clean;
- the bar rag flipped itself over an imaginary shoulder as if nodding to the party.

Fala and Tally witnessed the bar rag and shared what they knew of Trollskull Manor's history. The details are neighbor-memory and rumor, so preserve some uncertainty:

- Trollskull was the first establishment in the alley and gave the alley its name.
- The original owner was likely Ulkoria or Olkoria Stonemarrow; transcript spelling is uncertain.
- She left to adventure, having sold, lost, or otherwise given up the tavern depending on the story.
- A shield dwarf family later owned it and fell on hard times.
- Arissa Mirthkettle converted the building into an orphanage.
- Legend says Mirthkettle may have been a hag who cooked and ate orphans; some ghost stories still point to her or the children.
- After Mirthkettle was removed or killed, the building sat abandoned for about twenty years.
- A half-elf named Lif later reopened it as a tavern.
- Lif died roughly forty years ago when a support beam on ale casks broke and collapsed on him; he was not found for a day or two.
- Later owners, including the Morleys and perhaps the Curlbottoms, failed to make a lasting business of it.
- Emmet Frewn tried to buy the place, but Volo outbid him because of the ghost stories.

The neighbors disagree about whether the haunting is Mirthkettle, the orphanage children, Lif, or some mixture of stories. The ghost's bar behavior strongly supports Lif as the relevant current presence.

### Gazer Confirmation

Fala described the previous night's floating eye in more detail:

- It hovered in the street between Corellon's Crown and Trollskull Manor.
- It moved up to about the second story.
- It peered into the manor windows but also scanned the whole street.
- It looked like a gazer, similar to the one the party fought in the sewers.

This made the Trollskull surveillance feel more directly Xanathar-linked.

### Vajra's Report and Reward

Vajra contacted Jonathan by Sending and asked whether "that mess in the Dock Ward" was the party's doing. Jonathan answered evasively, forcing Vajra to ask more directly what they found. She then contacted Broethuul instead, asking for a report and, in a bit of exasperated humor, asking him to slap Jonathan.

Broethuul reported that the creature appeared to have been imported in an ironbound crate, with perhaps two more crates involved. Vajra replied:

- report received;
- preserve what the party has;
- do not hand the evidence to the Watch yet;
- it may be useful for a cleaner accounting;
- enjoy the opera, though they had earned less peace than they thought.

Soon after, a cloth pouch embroidered with Blackstaff Tower appeared on the bar with 30 gold dragons inside. The party treated it as payment and put it toward the tavern fund/roof repair rather than splitting it immediately.

### Missing and Returned Clues

The party reviewed who held the clues:

- The receipt/cargo chit had been found by Jonathan.
- The note fragment had been found by Aladeen, according to table memory in this session.
- Davy had found the ironbound crate at the warehouse.

Jonathan then realized the cargo chit was missing from his belongings. Aladeen realized the note fragment was also missing. The party suspected a theft or trick. After Tally and Fala left, Vajra contacted Davy by Sending about the address on the chit. The exact name she gave is not captured in the transcript; prep points to a chandlery/shipping-broker lead near Fishgut Court. After the Sending, the note and chit reappeared among the party's belongings.

## Trollskull Errands and Neighbor Visits

### Vincent Trench and Tiger's Eye

Aladeen visited [[Places/Tiger's Eye]], the private investigation office in the alley, and met [[People/NPCs/Vincent Trench]]. Vincent was sharply dressed, pipe-smoking, and looked like he had just woken up.

Aladeen asked about the gazer and whether Vincent could help identify who was watching Trollskull Manor. Vincent said:

- he had not been present when the gazer came through;
- Fala had told him about it earlier;
- a gazer used this way always leads back to Xanathar;
- he did not know which Xanathar person was responsible;
- he could potentially find out who was looking at the alley, for pay;
- he suspected the gazer was watching the party rather than the alley itself.

Aladeen did not formally hire him, but asked Vincent to warn the party if he saw anything around the alley.

### Rishaal and Book Wyrm's Treasure

Aladeen also visited [[Places/Book Wyrm's Treasure]], a bookshop run by [[People/NPCs/Rishaal the Page-Turner]]. Rishaal is a gold dragonborn with reading glasses, a crowded shop, and a strong welcome-neighbor instinct.

Aladeen asked for books on grells, gazers, aberrations, and perhaps ghosts. Rishaal loaned him:

- one useful copy of Volo's monster writing;
- another Volo printing, which was functionally redundant;
- an older leather-bound book written in Draconic.

Rishaal loaned the books as a welcome gift and asked that they be returned. The Volo book had a gazer image useful enough for the urchins to compare against what they saw. It did not appear to contain grell information. The Draconic book could not be read by Aladeen or Broethuul, though Broethuul recognized the script as Draconic.

Aladeen sent the urchins to buy bread with copper for dinner and told them to be careful with the borrowed book.

## Preparing for the Opera

The party cleaned up as best they could with buckets, rags, and the manor's limited facilities. They discussed opera etiquette:

- showing up fully armed for war would be frowned upon;
- noble-style open carry of a status weapon could be acceptable;
- concealed weapons might be possible, with risk if discovered.

Davy gave one of his daggers to the urchins rather than a bow or crossbow, telling them to use it if the eyeball came back. This should be tracked as an actual table event, even if adults may later regret it.

Broethuul lectured the upstairs ghost or denizens for failing to repair the roof, windows, and furniture, and demanded more pride in the manor. There was no obvious response.

### Threestrings' Formalwear

[[People/NPCs/Threestrings]] arrived later in the afternoon with bundles of clothes and accessories.

For Davy:

- a wildly gaudy stage-performance suit, more costume than fashion;
- a genuinely nice black felt hat;
- the outfit was bright, ruffled, sturdy, and attention-grabbing enough to help Davy disappear behind the clothes rather than inside them.

For Jonathan:

- a huge pendant of Helm's symbol, theatrical enough to scream "justice" from the back row.
- Jonathan accepted politely but did not put it on; Threestrings noticed and seemed a little hurt.

For Broethuul:

- a pointed Robin Hood-like felt hat with an extremely long feather, which Broethuul accepted happily.

For Elior:

- a bright yellow-green/chartreuse silk cravat, left for him because he was absent.

For Aladeen:

- dark blue crushed-velvet dandy slippers with pointed toes.

Threestrings said he also hoped to attend the opera, though he had a few prior engagements.

### Fala Saves Davy's Outfit

As the party left for the evening, Fala saw Davy's outfit and intervened. They pulled him into Corellon's Crown and, using tailoring skill plus magic, adjusted the suit:

- the worst lace and ruffles were hidden or tamed;
- the colors were brought into a single more reasonable blue;
- the result looked like something a minor noble or rich merchant might plausibly wear;
- the effect would last about eight hours.

Davy offered payment, but Fala refused, saying this was for the sake of the entire alley.
