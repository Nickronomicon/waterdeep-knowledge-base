# Session 05 - 2026-04-01

Session 5 began at sunset on Ches 2 with the party walking from [[Places/Trollskull Manor]] to [[Places/Blackstaff Tower]] after [[People/NPCs/Vajra Safahr|Vajra Safahr's]] summons. By the next morning, Ches 3, they had accepted probationary [[Factions/Gray Hands]] work, investigated a Dock Ward monster release, killed a [[Factions/Xanathar Guild|Xanathar-linked]] grell, rescued a dock worker, and fled before the Watch arrived. The session ended with two immediate obligations still live: report back to Vajra and attend the opera to meet [[People/NPCs/Mirt]].

## Start State

- The party is leaving Trollskull Manor at sundown on Ches 2, 1492 DR.
- They have the Mirt opera invitation for the following evening, Ches 3.
- [[People/NPCs/Threestrings]] is expected to help Davy with formalwear.
- Trollskull Manor is occupied but unrepaired, haunted, and watched by the urchins.

## Major Scenes

### Watched on the Way to Blackstaff Tower

On the walk through the Market toward Blackstaff Tower, several party members noticed coordinated surveillance:

- A cloaked figure and repeated faces appeared whenever the party paused or changed streets.
- A raven seemed to watch with person-like attention rather than ordinary bird behavior.
- A child announced "they're almost there" to someone unseen, then left.
- Broethuul approached one tail directly, spooking the watchers into keeping greater distance.
- The party tried to scatter and hide in the Market; Aladeen vanished effectively, while others hid with much less grace.

Vajra later denied that the watchers were hers, explaining that she has more arcane ways to observe people.

### Blackstaff Tower

The party entered Blackstaff Tower through a blank black-stone wall that opened only when they reached it. Inside, the tower was larger than its exterior, with floating lantern guidance, impossible stairs, windows onto other weather and places, repeating apprentices, changing doors, and a general sense that the building's interior was not obeying ordinary architecture.

The guiding lantern led the party to Vajra's chamber. Vajra greeted them with pointed familiarity:

- She addressed Broethuul as "Lord Broethuul" and referenced the Autumn Court.
- She acknowledged Jonathan as young Lord Eagleshield and offered condolences for the canceled Crommor engagement.
- She did not recognize Davy, which matters because of Davy's hidden identity.
- She knew Aladeen as the jester famous for mocking the Crommors.
- She addressed Elior as Lord Thalosmere and asked him to tell his uncle she would return something borrowed before another fortnight passed.

Aladeen took a blank sheet from Vajra's desk and began folding a paper airplane. Vajra, without much concern, took notes on the party's tailing incident, folded her own paper bird, and sent it away.

### The Gray Hands Offer

Vajra explained that Waterdeep's ordinary institutions are sufficient for ordinary problems, but not for threats that move too quickly, strangely, or sensitively for courts and guardsmen. She is restoring the tradition remembered as Force Grey through the [[Factions/Gray Hands]], her irregular agents.

Key terms from the offer:

- The Gray Hands are not guards, constables, or salaried soldiers.
- Members act under Vajra's authority when Waterdeep needs skill, judgment, and discretion.
- Work is paid when work is done, but the arrangement is service, not employment.
- Invoking the Blackstaff's name carries weight, but it is not something to advertise casually.
- Vajra expects competence, restraint, loyalty to Waterdeep, and "a solution, not a spectacle."
- She accepts that members may also belong to other factions or have their own lives.
- She communicates mostly through Sending and summons people to the tower when needed.

Jonathan accepted almost immediately, recognizing Force Grey from heroic stories. Broethuul accepted after confirming that irregular service would still allow him to be away in the wilds or beyond easy reach. Aladeen joined with theatrical enthusiasm. Elior pressed for what the group receives in exchange; Vajra emphasized resources, access, and service to Waterdeep rather than transactional reward. Davy appears to have gone along with the group, though the transcript does not preserve a distinct acceptance speech.

The party is best treated as probationary Gray Hands after this session, pending Vajra's judgment on the Dock Ward assignment.

### Dock Ward Assignment

Vajra explained that Xanathar likes to release monsters to spread panic and pull the Watch thin. There had been a Dock Ward disturbance the previous night or night before, around the same period as the party's Floon/Renaer rescue, and she suspected it might happen again soon.

She gave the party a first assignment:

- Investigate the Dock Ward disturbance.
- Find out what the creature was and what happened.
- End it cleanly if they can.
- Begin at first light, because witnesses lie more clearly in daylight and less drunkenly after the taverns empty.

Before leaving, Aladeen asked whether Vajra could send magically handy apprentices to clean Trollskull Manor. Vajra's advice was to "make use of the energy inside already," pointing them toward cooperation with the manor's haunting rather than outside labor.

### Night at Trollskull

The trip back from Blackstaff Tower was uneventful; the watchers were gone or hidden.

At Trollskull Manor, [[People/NPCs/Nat]], [[People/NPCs/Jenks]], and [[People/NPCs/Squiddly]] were huddled on the front steps and frightened. They reported:

- A weird floating eye thing had come by.
- A man asked whether the party was home.
- Someone tried the upstairs window or latch.
- The urchins heard the window rattle, retreated inside, then became unsure whether inside was safer.

The creature sounded like a gazer or similar eye-creature. Broethuul checked the building's doors and windows as best he could, but Trollskull remains a tavern, not a fortress.

The party then shared more personal background:

- Elior learned that Broethuul's noble title is not just a bit; Broethuul described the Autumn Court, seven houses, betrayal, the attack on his keep, and swimming into Waterdeep through the harbor.
- Vajra's mention of Jonathan's canceled Crommor engagement prompted party discussion of Anastasia Crommor, the failed Eagleshield-Crommor alliance, and Aladeen's Crommor Collapse routine.
- Davy deflected questions about noble resentment by explaining that street survival did not leave much time to think about noble houses.

Broethuul also addressed Trollskull Manor itself, declaring the party's intent to restore the tavern and asking the house's unseen "denizens" to help. In response, old tankards set themselves upright, dust blew out of them, and Threestrings' brandy poured itself into mugs for the party. The bottle then tilted as if an unseen presence took a drink. The party toasted the manor and likely made meaningful contact with [[People/NPCs/Lif]], though his name remains unrevealed in table truth.

### Ches 3 Morning

The urchins woke early and noisily practiced fighting downstairs. The DM explicitly dated the day as Ches 3, 1492 DR.

Vajra sent Jonathan another Sending: in the Dock Ward, they should speak to [[People/NPCs/Ma Sella]], a fishwife and the best witness known so far. Jonathan asked, in earnest, which fish she was married to. Vajra eventually spent the reply to explain that Ma Sella sells fish and is not married.

The party headed to the Dock Ward, with Aladeen keeping watch for tails. No watchers were detected on the way.

### Ma Sella's Witness Account

Ma Sella was easy to find because she is a Dock Ward fixture. Broethuul asked which fish was her husband; she almost took offense, then realized the question was sincere.

Her useful account:

- The creature dropped from a warehouse roof.
- It had tentacles, a beak, and floated.
- It snatched someone up.
- Two rough, violent men nearby were shouting at it as if it should obey them.
- She saw no obvious Zhentarim marks, but the men were muscle rather than street folk, dock people, or fishermen.
- The creature may have gone into sewers, over the harbor, back into warehouses, or elsewhere; rumors disagree wildly.

Broethuul bought a whole fresh mackerel from her for six silver and put it in his bag. This is now a campaign fact.

### The Fish Street Warehouse

The party investigated the warehouse Ma Sella identified, near Fish Street/Fishgut Court. It was conspicuously closed while other nearby warehouses were working.

Initial evidence:

- The building was abandoned or inactive despite being valuable Dock Ward real estate.
- A storm-drain style sewer grate about twenty feet from the warehouse was bent upward and damaged.
- Tool-like marks and interior scrape marks suggested something may have forced its way through or been released through the drain.
- Nearby workers had only wild rumors: dragon, aboleth, demon, and other incorrect guesses.

The party split:

- Elior and Jonathan went to the Guild of Scriveners to identify the warehouse owner.
- Aladeen, Davy, and Broethuul remained at the warehouse.

At the guild, a clerk named [[People/NPCs/Andrew]] pulled public property records for two silver, then discovered the target warehouse's ownership chain had been neatly redacted. He did not charge for the failed lookup. Off the record, he explained that such redaction usually means Closed Lord business, Xanathar influence, Zhentarim influence, or some combination. Elior understood that the missing record was suspicious but not impossible in Waterdeep; the record likely exists beyond a clerk's access.

At the warehouse, Davy picked the side door lock while Aladeen and Broethuul screened him from passersby. Inside, they saw an unconscious or dying dock worker and a floating brain-like creature with a beak and tentacles: later identified as a grell.

### Grell at the Warehouse

Davy and Aladeen briefly backed away, but Broethuul walked in and drew the grell's attention. The fight and containment unfolded messily:

- Broethuul shot the grell with a crossbow from the doorway.
- The grell grabbed the injured dock worker and began moving toward the door.
- Aladeen cast Sleep, incapacitating the grell long enough to shut the door.
- The wounded worker woke and screamed under the sleeping creature.
- The party opened the door to let him out, then tried to shut the door again.
- One thug inside blocked the door with his boot; Broethuul stabbed the foot, forcing him back.
- Aladeen re-chained the door without fully locking it.
- The grell woke and attacked the two handlers/thugs still trapped inside.
- Jonathan and Elior returned from the guild while the sounds inside became increasingly awful.
- Jonathan healed the rescued dock worker, [[People/NPCs/Armet]], restoring him from near death.
- The party debated calling the Watch versus proving themselves to Vajra.
- The grell broke the door open. The party used readied actions against it.
- Aladeen attempted Bane, but the grell resisted.
- Jonathan missed with his morningstar.
- Broethuul hit with another crossbow bolt.
- Davy missed with a crossbow shot.
- Elior's Ice Knife missed the direct strike but exploded, injuring Jonathan and killing the grell.

The grell had apparently killed or partly consumed the two rough men inside the warehouse. The party saved Armet but not the handlers.

### Clues Recovered

The party searched quickly before the Watch could arrive:

- On one ruined body, they found a torn [[Objects/Dockside Transfer Receipt]] for multiple ironbound crates.
- In the warehouse, they found an [[Objects/Ironbound Grell Crate|ironbound crate]] cracked open to roughly grell size.
- Near the crate, they found [[Objects/Loose It Then Leave Note Fragment|a mostly burned note fragment]] reading: "Loose it, then leave."

The receipt handout indicates three ironbound crates, no customs inspection, silver payment, and a mark of one staring eye inside a ring. The transcript revealed the plurality and ironbound-crate nature clearly; use the handout for the exact player-facing clue text.

Session 6 table memory clarified clue custody as Jonathan with the receipt, Aladeen with the note fragment, and Davy most associated with finding the crate. The exact recovery order in the session 5 transcript remains muddied.

Armet said he remembered little: someone disappeared two nights ago, the boss told him to get the warehouse running that morning, and he woke with a tentacle in his stomach and a brain floating over him. His employer was "Jonas and Co," which did not mean anything to the party at the table.

Broethuul persuaded Armet that he had never seen the party. Armet, grateful and terrified, agreed and stumbled away.

## End State

- The grell is dead in the Fish Street warehouse.
- Two apparent handlers/thugs are dead inside the warehouse.
- Armet survived and was healed by Jonathan.
- The party fled before the Watch arrived.
- The party has evidence of multiple ironbound crates, a burned instruction note, and likely Xanathar-linked monster release activity.
- The party still needs to report back to Vajra.
- The opera at [[Places/Lightsinger Theater]] and meeting with [[People/NPCs/Mirt]] are still upcoming.
