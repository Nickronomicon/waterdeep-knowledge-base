# Session 09 - 2026-05-07

Session 9 began in the mid-afternoon of Ches 4, 1492 DR, after the party returned from the [[Places/Thomril & Sons Cartage]] fire smelling of smoke and carrying the information [[People/NPCs/Mirt]] had asked them to recover from [[People/NPCs/Maxeene]]. The session moved from Harper report-back at [[Places/Ulbrinter Villa]] into a new Trollskull haunting lead: [[People/NPCs/Dorgar]], the hidden [[Places/Last Temple of Myrkul]], and the first steps toward a seance or spirit contact at [[Places/Trollskull Manor]].

## Starting State

- [[People/NPCs/Maxeene]] was alive and looking for new work after Thomril & Sons burned.
- The stablemaster/owner, likely [[People/NPCs/Whelan Thomril]], was killed in the arson fight.
- [[People/NPCs/Thugby]] escaped alive again after recognizing the party.
- The party had the session-9 table handout summarizing Maxeene's information.
- The party had not been told by Mirt exactly whom to report to after the Maxeene job.

## Revealed Handouts

- `../raw/notes/handouts/s9-Maxeene-info.md`: revealed to all players in session 9 as a recap of the information gained from Maxeene.
- `../raw/notes/handouts/s9-thalosmere-archive-report.md`: revealed privately to Elior before or during session 9, representing a House Thalosmere archive profile on [[People/NPCs/Remallia Haventree]].

## Trollskull Return and Ulbrinter Calling Card

- The party returned to [[Places/Trollskull Manor]] in the mid-afternoon, smelling of burned hay and wood smoke.
- [[People/NPCs/Nat]], [[People/NPCs/Jenks]], and [[People/NPCs/Squiddly]] rushed out to report that a "fancy man" had visited.
- The visitor wore a proper tailed coat, smelled heavily of perfume, and left a calling card bearing the Ulbrinter crest.
- The card requested that the party visit at their "earliest convenience," which noble PCs understood as "come immediately."
- Nat distrusted the messenger because he smiled too much and smelled too strongly of perfume.
- Squiddly had lifted the messenger's embroidered handkerchief, also marked with the Ulbrinter crest.
- Elior recognized that [[People/NPCs/Remallia Haventree]], widow of Lord Arthagast Ulbrinter, lived at Ulbrinter Villa and had recently appeared publicly at the opera after roughly five years of seclusion.

## Preparing for Ulbrinter Villa

- The party took a short rest and cleaned themselves up enough to visit a noble household.
- Elior changed into old formalwear that was nice but dated and perhaps slightly too small.
- Jonathan changed into a clean version of his usual tabard/cloak presentation.
- Davy put on the now-muted formal outfit he had been darkening after the opera.
- Aladeen changed into a clean, presentable casual outfit and dropped off the extra weapons.
- The urchin armory now appears to include a dagger for Nat, a dagger for Squiddly, and the mace for Jenks.
- The group warned the children to be careful, with the practical note that they had bread money but not doctor money.

## Ulbrinter Villa

[[Places/Ulbrinter Villa]] was quiet rather than abandoned: ivy on old stone, clean windows, drawn curtains, and servants moving with disciplined precision. The perfumed, handkerchiefless servant who had delivered the card escorted the party to a pale morning room overlooking a bare garden.

Remallia was composed, grave, and much more controlled than she had appeared at the opera.

- She opened directly: Mirt had told her the party spoke with Maxeene, and she wanted Maxeene's exact words, not impressions or conclusions.
- When asked about Mirt, Remallia described him as someone who works for the city in his own roundabout way, concerned with recruitment, finance, and keeping Waterdeep safe.
- Aladeen displayed his Harper pin; Remallia watched without reacting.
- Aladeen and Elior noticed subtle Harper symbolism repeated in the room's carved trim. The morning room may function as a discreet Harper meeting room or shrine.
- Elior drew on the private Thalosmere archive report and public noble knowledge: Remallia has a benevolent reputation, old charitable ties, and a long recent withdrawal after her husband's death.

## Report to Remallia

The party reported the Maxeene information:

- Maxeene gave a ride one or two days earlier to a sun elf and a half-orc bodyguard.
- They were dropped at [[Places/Yawning Portal]].
- They discussed hiring enough spies to root out Xanathar Guild hideouts in the city.
- The half-orc description strongly matched [[People/NPCs/Yagra Stonefist]], and the sun elf likely matched [[People/NPCs/Davil Starsong]].
- Remallia agreed this was reasonable conjecture but cautioned them not to treat second-hand description as confirmed names.

The party also reported Maxeene's Stone-adjacent rock gnome lead:

- A rock gnome had hired her dray to follow a lord's carriage while talking to himself about a precious stone.
- The gnome later hired her again to follow a well-dressed red-haired noble or gentleman on foot.
- The gnome kept trying to slow the driver so they would not overtake the target.
- Remallia said the Stone business was new to her and sent a paper bird immediately.

The party then reported the Thomril & Sons attack:

- The attackers had been looking for "old man Mirt's spy."
- Remallia concluded someone was followed, and called that unfortunate but informative.
- Broethuul named Thugby as a recurring ruffian involved both in the Castle Ward aftermath and the Thomril & Sons arson crew.
- Remallia did not know Thugby, but sent the perfumed servant to learn everything about him by the end of the day.

The party also revealed [[Objects/Scrap of Note]] to Remallia:

- Remallia had not previously been told about the note.
- She cautioned against rushing to assumptions, but accepted that X and Z as Xanathar and Zhentarim were reasonable guesses.
- She had no immediate candidate for "Lady B."
- The crossover between criminal gangs and noble houses made her suspect the beginning of another Great Game in Waterdeep.

## Remallia's Guidance and Support

- Remallia said the best thing the party could do now was get [[Places/Trollskull Manor]] open.
- She described taverns as the mouthpiece of a city and emphasized the need for information.
- She declined to become a formal silent partner or direct lender because the party should not be officially tied to her.
- She gave the party [[Objects/Remallia's Fala Credit Note]], a note to show [[People/NPCs/Fala Lefaliir]] if they need medicine and cannot immediately pay.
- She gave Aladeen a one-use [[Objects/Remallia's Paper Bird]] for contacting her or Mirt, warning that fewer words are safer.
- She provided [[Objects/Remallia's Lender List]], a short list of possible lenders or hospitality-adjacent contacts. The only name that jumped out to the party was [[People/NPCs/Victoro Cassalanter]].
- She asked that the party not mention her name to anyone on the lender list.
- The cover story for the visit is that Remallia tried to hire the city's current darling adventurers to clear stirges from a chimney, but it turned out to be only a nest of crows.
- When leaving, Remallia asked the party to call her Remy.

## The Ulbrinter Servant

- The same elderly, perfumed servant escorted the party out after replacing his missing handkerchief.
- He said it is rare for Lady Remallia to receive guests and rarer to receive them twice.
- He hoped the party would remain good friends to her ladyship.
- A successful read suggested he is deeply in love with Remallia, likely a long-standing and unrequited devotion.

## Rest of the Day

After leaving Ulbrinter Villa, the DM confirmed it was late afternoon on Ches 4, 1492 DR. The party considered financing and remembered that Victoro Cassalanter had already invited them to call at Cassalanter Villa within the next tenday.

Broethuul then proposed finding an enchanter or spiritualist who could help the party speak with Trollskull Manor's ghosts. His theory was that Maxeene demonstrated the value of unnoticed listeners, and ghosts in a tavern could become a potent information network if the party could communicate with them.

## The Plinth and Brother Senric

The party went to [[Places/The Plinth]], the ruined all-faiths temple south of the City of the Dead. Elior used religious knowledge to identify likely approaches:

- Mystra or Oghma for magic/knowledge.
- Kelemvor for the dead.
- Avoid Lathander if the party did not want undead-hating attention on the manor.

The party approached the Kelemvor altar and met [[People/NPCs/Brother Senric]], an eager young acolyte in his second year at the Plinth and third year serving Kelemvor.

- Elior offered his aging mackerel as a gift, to Senric's polite confusion and the distress of nearby noses.
- Broethuul asked about speaking with lingering spirits who had died but remained present.
- Senric insisted the dead should rest and offered help arranging an exorcist from the Temple of Kelemvor.
- Broethuul argued that the Trollskull spirit might enjoy continuing its old routines; Senric framed that as potentially hollow repetition, like an addiction to life.
- Elior was persuaded by Senric's theological argument and shifted toward wanting to help the ghosts move on.
- Broethuul loudly called for necromancy help, alarming Senric and drawing some attention.
- Senric admitted he had never performed an exorcism and could not actually speak with spirits.
- The party left him one silver as a donation; the coin audibly hit the empty bottom of the donation box.

## Old Xoblob Shop

The party visited [[Places/Old Xoblob Shop]] to ask [[People/NPCs/Xoblob]] about speaking with ghosts.

- Xoblob welcomed Broethuul back and asked whether he had reconsidered selling an eye.
- Xoblob said he does not hear voices or speak with ghosts, but suggested a warlock or necromancer might know how.
- He warned against looking openly for a necromancer, especially if the party has any Watch connections.
- Aladeen inspected a bright purple lute in the shop. It was out of tune but well made, not magical, and offered at 60 gp before being bargained down to 45 gp.
- Aladeen did not buy the lute.
- Davy then said he knew someone and led the party toward the [[Places/City of the Dead]].

## Dorgar and the Last Temple of Myrkul

At the City of the Dead near evening, Davy warmly greeted [[People/NPCs/Dorgar]], who was cleaning headstones.

- Dorgar is tall, gaunt, pale, and terrifying in appearance, with a voice like stone dragged across concrete.
- Despite his corpse-like look, he greeted Davy warmly and was delighted that Davy had a place to live.
- Davy introduced Dorgar as a dear friend and explained that the party had inherited Trollskull Manor and wanted to communicate with its ghosts.
- The party told Dorgar they believed there may be multiple ghosts: a barkeep, possibly other tavern staff, and something less friendly upstairs.
- Dorgar asked if Davy trusted the party. Davy said yes, and Dorgar therefore trusted them.
- Dorgar required everyone to promise that what they were about to see would not leave the grounds.

Dorgar led the party to a hidden stair behind a mausoleum and down to [[Places/Last Temple of Myrkul]], the last hidden Myrkul temple in Waterdeep.

- The entry bears Myrkul's symbol: a skull inside a triangle.
- Elior recognized the symbol as Myrkul, god of death rather than god of the dead.
- The chamber is simple, with a bare altar, black altar cloth, repeated skull relief, and a side passage.
- Dorgar said the other Myrkul temples were destroyed by followers of the Lightbringer.
- Dorgar can possibly speak with a ghost, but the ghost must want to speak with him; he cannot compel it.
- He gave the party two ordinary-looking off-white [[Objects/Dorgar's Seance Candles]].
- Instructions: light the first candle in a window at Trollskull Manor, burn it completely down, use its flame to light the second, and burn the second completely down. The process takes about a day; Dorgar recommends starting in an evening and finishing the next evening.
- Once both candles have burned, Dorgar can make the remaining preparations.
- Dorgar also warned that many hauntings are not actually ghosts; they can be other magical phenomena.
- Dorgar agreed to come to Trollskull Manor for Davy.

## End State

- It is evening on Ches 4, 1492 DR.
- The Maxeene report has been delivered to [[People/NPCs/Remallia Haventree]], who appears to be a senior Harper or Harper-adjacent handler.
- Remallia is investigating the Stone lead, the Scrap of Note, and Thugby.
- The party has medicine credit with Fala, a one-use paper bird to contact Remallia or Mirt, and a lender list that includes Victoro Cassalanter.
- The party is now pursuing direct communication with Trollskull Manor's haunting.
- Dorgar's seance preparation requires roughly one day of candle-burning at Trollskull Manor before he can attempt contact.
- Session ended at the hidden Myrkul temple, before the party returned home or began burning the candles.

## Open Questions

- What does Remallia learn from the Stone lead and the note?
- Who is "Lady B," and does Remallia's Great Game concern prove accurate?
- What does the Ulbrinter servant learn about Thugby?
- Will the party call on Victoro Cassalanter for a loan before or after the seance preparations?
- Are the Trollskull phenomena actually ghosts, and if so, who will agree to speak through Dorgar?
- How will Elior's newly expressed interest in exorcising the ghosts interact with Broethuul's plan to employ them as tavern spies?
- What risk does the hidden Myrkul temple create for Davy and the party if discovered?
