# Session 10 - 2026-06-11

Session 10 began the morning after [[Sessions/Session 09 - 2026-05-07]], on Ches 5, 1492 DR. The party returned safely from the [[Places/Last Temple of Myrkul]] offscreen, began [[Objects/Dorgar's Seance Candles|Dorgar's candle preparation]] at [[Places/Trollskull Manor]], visited neighborhood craftspeople, negotiated [[Objects/Cassalanter Loan|a Cassalanter loan]], and ended before the Underbough trial dinner began in earnest.

## Starting State

- The party had returned from the hidden Myrkul temple.
- [[People/NPCs/Dorgar]] had promised to help with the Trollskull haunting once the candle procedure was completed.
- [[People/NPCs/Remallia Haventree]] had given the party [[Objects/Remallia's Paper Bird]], [[Objects/Remallia's Fala Credit Note]], and [[Objects/Remallia's Lender List]].
- [[People/NPCs/Victoro Cassalanter]] remained the obvious financing lead for Trollskull Manor.
- The session 10 prep expected the Cassalanter loan, dinner with the Underboughs, signing, and seance; actual play reached the loan negotiation and the start of the Underbough dinner setup, but not the signing or seance.

## Morning at Trollskull

- The DM confirmed it was the fifth of Ches.
- The urchins were loudly mock-fighting downstairs with the weapons the party had given them: two daggers and a mace.
- [[People/PCs/Broethuul]] noticed that [[People/PCs/Elior Thalosmere]] was missing. Elior's room was empty, perfectly ordered, and showed no sign of struggle.
- [[People/PCs/Davy]] began tending Dorgar's candles. The first candle was lit in an upstairs window because the lower windows remained boarded.
- The candle process was clarified at the table: each candle burns roughly eight hours, and the whole preparation takes about 24 hours before Dorgar can come to do his work.
- Elior's absence was handled privately with the DM.

## Steam and Steel

[[People/PCs/Aladeen]], [[People/PCs/Jonathan Usman Sonny Theodore Icarus Charles Eagleshield|Jonathan]], the urchins, and possibly others visited [[Places/Steam and Steel]], the neighborhood forge.

- The forge is run by [[People/NPCs/Embric]], a fire genasi weaponsmith, and [[People/NPCs/Avi]], a water genasi armorsmith.
- Embric was forging a huge sword, with flame wreathing his arm on each hammer strike.
- Avi was tending the forge around a large shield and happily explained armor and shield craft.
- Embric and Avi had not seen Elior.
- Aladeen sold a spear for 4 sp and left the value as credit on a Trollskull account.
- Aladeen considered studded leather and child-sized training shields, but did not buy armor or shields here.
- Embric and Avi are interested in drinking at Trollskull once it opens.

## The Bent Nail

The group then visited [[Places/The Bent Nail]] and [[People/NPCs/Tally Fellbranch]].

- Tally was working on a commissioned dining-room table for a Castle Ward client.
- Aladeen asked for light wooden bucklers for the urchins.
- Tally agreed to make three wooden training bucklers for 10 gp total, ready the next day.
- The bucklers are intended for practice and child safety, not as mechanically meaningful combat shields.
- After the Cassalanter meeting, the party stopped back by The Bent Nail and told Tally to come by because Trollskull would soon have work for him.

## Elior's Private Errand

Elior returned alone to [[Places/The Plinth]] to pursue a Kelemvorite exorcism option.

- [[People/NPCs/Brother Senric]] was not present; a different solemn Kelemvorite representative was staffing the altar.
- Elior said Trollskull Manor had undead or a poltergeist that should move on.
- The Kelemvorite representative agreed an exorcism could be arranged but explained that a donation affected priority.
- Elior offered 5 gp, which was accepted but treated as a low-priority donation for a nonviolent poltergeist problem.
- Elior tried to impress the urgency by saying his friends were attempting to materialize or commune with the ghost in a way offensive to Kelemvor.
- The representative did not refuse aid, but explained that serious harmful undead would take priority.
- Elior requested literature and received suggested titles.

## Elior's Research Books

Elior gathered [[Objects/Elior's Ghost Research Books]]:

- *Dead and Beyond*, available from House Thalosmere.
- *Domain of Death and Other Esoteric Topics*, found in the deeper Thalosmere archives.
- *Volo's Guide to the Undead*, newly released and purchased for 12 gp 5 sp.

Elior returned to Trollskull and began reading. When the party asked what he had been doing, he described it as family-history and self-knowledge research rather than saying outright that he was seeking exorcism options.

## Cassalanter Villa

Aladeen, Broethuul, and Jonathan went to [[Places/Cassalanter Villa]] to pursue financing. Davy stayed with the candles, and Elior stayed at Trollskull reading.

- The villa was placed at the table on Delzorin Street in the Sea Ward, though earlier notes placed it on Diamond Street; keep this location discrepancy pending verification.
- Cassalanter Villa has stark white walls, a gleaming crimson roof and turrets, green gardens, hedges, water features, a tall white brick wall, and a single wrought-iron gate.
- The Cassalanter crest on the gate was described as a green Y overlapping a stylized goose being fed.
- Two armored guards were stationed at the gate.
- Jonathan opened poorly by grabbing the gate and demanding entry; the guards called the visitors peasants and moved to block him.
- Jonathan invoked the Eagleshield name, and Aladeen smoothed the introduction by naming the party's noble and notable connections.
- The party was admitted after a servant checked inside.

## Loan Negotiation

[[People/NPCs/Victoro Cassalanter]] received the party in a salon after greeting them as Renaer's heroes and Floon's rescuers.

- Victoro's voice and presence were notably smooth, rich, and persuasive.
- The party explained the repair needs, guild obligations, furniture, staffing, and initial stock.
- The group estimated roughly 1,500 gp would be needed to open.
- Victoro suggested opening by the festival period around Ches 19-21, especially the large waterdeep seafaring celebration on Ches 21, as a way to relaunch Trollskull Manor with immediate foot traffic.
- Victoro offered funds the next day and said repayment would not begin until after Trollskull opened.
- Initial terms discussed: 1,500 gp at 3% per tenday after opening.
- Broethuul negotiated for an introductory 2% rate for the first three months.
- Victoro instead pushed the principal to 2,000 gp, saying the party needed more than they thought.
- Broethuul countered with four months at 2% for the 2,000 gp principal, then 3% thereafter.
- Victoro agreed.
- Terms: 2,000 gp principal; 2% per tenday for the first four months after opening; 3% per tenday thereafter; interest begins the first tenday after opening; principal repayment is separate.
- Victoro will send the money and paperwork to Trollskull Manor the next day, with all parties required to sign.
- Victoro will contact the relevant guilds and have representatives present so they can be paid immediately.
- The meeting ended with an expensive brandy toast to business.

## Returning to Trollskull

On the way back, the party stopped at The Bent Nail and told Tally to come by because work would soon be available.

At Trollskull, two halflings were waiting outside with a covered basket, bedrolls, traveling gear, and hopeful patience:

- [[People/NPCs/Corvin Underbough]], a prospective cook.
- [[People/NPCs/Miri Underbough]], his sister and sous chef.

They had heard Trollskull might reopen as a public house and came seeking work.

## Underbough Trial Dinner Setup

- The party invited Corvin and Miri inside to show what they could do.
- Aladeen gave Corvin 1 gp to buy ingredients for dinner.
- Corvin and Miri inspected the pantry and kitchen, discovering that the pantry was a total loss and the kitchen was barely usable.
- Corvin left to shop for ingredients.
- Aladeen made a Help Wanted sign for a barman and barmaid, using charcoal on scrap wood. His performance roll was strong, so the sign is likely effective.

## Kitchen Haunting

While Corvin was away, Miri encountered the kitchen haunting.

- Miri shrieked and fled behind the bar after seeing pots and pans move in the kitchen.
- Broethuul treated the haunting as a potential cook or kitchen staff member and brought Miri back in.
- Broethuul addressed the ghost as a famous chef, explaining that the Underboughs were being tested as kitchen staff and asking the ghost to let them cook, helping only if asked.
- Writing appeared in grime on the kitchen wall: "I think we've got rats."
- Miri was terrified, but Broethuul and Aladeen reassured her that the ghost was trying to help.
- Miri asked whether she could wait outside until Corvin returned.

## End State

- It is still Ches 5, 1492 DR.
- Dorgar's candle preparation has begun at Trollskull Manor and should complete the next day if uninterrupted.
- The Cassalanter loan has been negotiated but not signed.
- Victoro will send the funds, documents, and relevant guild representatives to Trollskull Manor the next day.
- Tally is making three wooden training bucklers for the urchins, ready the next day.
- Corvin and Miri Underbough are in the middle of a trial dinner setup; dinner itself has not happened yet.
- The Trollskull kitchen haunting has warned, "I think we've got rats."
- The planned dinner, loan signing, and seance all slide to session 11.
