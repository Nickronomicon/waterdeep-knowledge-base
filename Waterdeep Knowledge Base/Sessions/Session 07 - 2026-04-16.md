# Session 07 - 2026-04-16

Session 7 covered the party's evening at [[Places/Lightsinger Theater]] on Ches 3, 1492 DR. Elior rejoined on the way, the party mingled with Waterdeep's elite, accepted association with the [[Factions/Harpers]] from [[People/NPCs/Mirt]], received the Maxeene mission, spotted a watcher, chased him into the Castle Ward back streets, survived an ambush, and recovered [[Objects/Scrap of Note|a strange note]] about the Stone and multiple factions.

The session ended after combat with one surrendered ambusher, several dead attackers, a dead apparent employer called [[People/NPCs/Mr Jones]], and one fleeing survivor who escaped into the sewers. The party has not yet visited Maxeene.

## Start State

- The party is leaving [[Places/Trollskull Alley]] for the Lightsinger Theater.
- [[People/NPCs/Threestrings]] has delivered opera clothes and accessories; [[People/NPCs/Fala Lefaliir]] magically corrected Davy's outfit.
- Elior was away speaking with his father during session 6, but catches up on the way to the theater.
- Davy receives or shares a private warning that the party is absolutely being watched, though he cannot tell from where.

## Arrival at Lightsinger Theater

The theater is in the Castle Ward, just off High Street. The party passed old-money carriages, liveried footmen, jewel-bright nobles, masked socialites, sugared-nut and hot-wine hawkers, banners of midnight blue and gold, and a facade of white stone with a stained-glass celestial choir oculus.

Aladeen had the tickets, swapped into his blue velvet slippers, and presented them at the door. The doorman was initially weary of the party's outfits but became more respectful once he saw the quality of their seats and had an usher show them inside.

Inside, the party found box-attendee-level hospitality: gilt balconies, velvet drapery, crystal lamps, perfume, powder, wine, polished marble, and rich people pretending not to inspect one another.

## Foyer Mingling

### Renaer and Floon

The party found [[People/NPCs/Renaer Neverember]] and [[People/NPCs/Floon Blagmaar]] in the pre-opera crowd. Floon was drunk, delighted, and loud about introducing the party as his saviors. He bought gin-and-tonics for the group. These drinks were magically dramatic: after drinking, hair and clothing fluttered in a theatrical breeze for about an hour, and the alcohol was stronger than ordinary alcohol.

Elior told Renaer about the party's trouble restoring [[Places/Trollskull Manor]] and dealing with guilds. The party also mentioned surveillance and the gazer around Trollskull Alley. Renaer was confused by the party's reference to "our children," which the party clarified as the three street urchins they had taken in.

### Notable Guests

The party recognized or was introduced to several important attendees:

- [[People/NPCs/Vajra Safahr]] was present across the room and nodded to the party.
- [[People/NPCs/Victoro Cassalanter]] and [[People/NPCs/Ammalia Cassalanter]] were present.
- [[People/NPCs/Orond Gralhund]] and [[People/NPCs/Yalah Gralhund]] were present; Yalah was hanging close to Ammalia and seeking her attention.
- [[People/NPCs/Margo Verida]], a singer known as the Siren of the Dales, attended but was not performing.
- [[People/NPCs/Zardoz Zord]], a flamboyant Luskan ship captain or entertainer with a naval air, attended with Margo.
- [[People/NPCs/Remallia Haventree]] appeared in public for the first time in roughly five years, an important social event by itself.
- [[People/NPCs/Laeral Silverhand]] arrived shortly before the opera began, accompanied by an expensive-looking older man later identifiable as Mirt, plus attendants and guards.

Elior approached Margo and Zardoz. Zardoz introduced himself, expected recognition, said he was from Luskan, and spoke of being in Waterdeep for the festival season with the [[Places/Sea Maidens Fair]]. The party remembered that three ships had arrived recently for the coming festival season and that the city was buzzing about what they would show off.

### The Cassalanter Loan Hook

Victoro and Ammalia Cassalanter approached after Floon's loud praise drew attention. Victoro recognized Jonathan and perhaps Elior, but mostly came because he had heard rumors of Renaer's saviors. He shook hands with the human men, gave Broethuul an odd look, and seemed to overlook Davy as too young to matter.

When the party mentioned owning Trollskull Manor and considering investors, Victoro praised the building's potential and said the Cassalanters do not usually invest in individual ventures, but could help with a loan. He invited the party to stop by [[Places/Cassalanter Villa]] on Diamond Street in the Sea Ward to discuss details.

Ammalia made a strong impression of noble presence and bearing. An older tiefling woman in black dress and headdress stood behind her, apparently an attendant, but was not introduced.

## The Opera

The party's actual seats were good but not noble-box good: a strong view from a wealthy merchant or upper-middle-class section rather than the elite boxes.

The opera was *The Fall of Tiamat*, sung in Giant. No one in the party spoke Giant. The performance looked impressive, full of lacquered masks, thunder sheets, crimson silk, gold-painted scenery, and theatrical giant acting. Broethuul knew enough of the story from the other side of the veil to suspect the opera was either very wrong or perhaps a deliberate farce.

During the first act, Broethuul noticed a theatergoer a few rows over and closer to the boxes who was not watching the show. The watcher seemed focused on Box C and occasionally on the party. Insight suggested he did not belong at the opera and was not there for art or social climbing.

## Box C and the Harpers

At intermission, an usher came to the party's seats and told them they were expected in Private Box C. On the way, Jonathan confirmed the watcher was still watching the box and getting more interested in the party.

Inside Box C, the party met [[People/NPCs/Mirt]], a broad aging man in expensive, sumptuous, not especially tasteful clothes, heavy rings, good brandy, and the air of someone who collects secrets. He did not rise, invited the party to sit, and framed them as "Renaer's good bets."

Mirt offered association with the [[Factions/Harpers]]. Key points of his pitch:

- The Harpers are not a second City Watch, a public crusading order, or a moral lecture.
- They interfere when the wrong people gather too much power and the right people are in danger of being buried by it.
- They value judgment, discretion, quiet action, and knowing which thread to pull without bringing the whole tapestry down.
- They ask questions, listen, pass information quietly, help the vulnerable when institutions fail, and counter tyrants, criminals, and noble fools.
- He offered acquaintance, information, work worth doing, and pay, but not obedience, a uniform, or a day job.
- "Once a Harper, always a Harper" was explained more as enduring alignment and network than military command.

All five PCs accepted Harper association:

- Elior picked up a pin and pocketed it.
- Aladeen took a pin with a flourish, making it vanish into his pocket.
- Davy silently palmed a pin; Mirt saw the whole thing and remarked on the silent ones.
- Jonathan accepted after the broader explanation of Harper goals.
- Broethuul asked whether the Harpers work beyond Waterdeep, what happens if a Harper is attacked on other business, and how Mirt views the Unseelie or necessary evils. Mirt said Waterdeep is only one chapter, Harper aid follows Harper identity, and he would likely draw a hard line at evil means even for good outcomes. Broethuul then accepted the last pin.

### First Harper Job: Maxeene

Mirt then gave the party their first Harper job:

- There is a Harper contact working with the Teamsters.
- Her name is Maxeene.
- Mirt believes she recently overheard useful talk from Zhentarim operatives.
- The party should find her, convince her they speak for the Harpers, and learn what she knows.
- Mirt framed the real test as whether the party knows how to ask the right questions of the right witness.

The transcript records some uncertainty around the exact business name during Mirt's briefing, but the paired prep identifies the intended location as [[Places/Thomril & Sons Cartage]].

## The Watcher and Chase

As the party left Box C, Aladeen noticed the watcher set down his glass and make for a backstage side passage. The party gave chase.

Backstage, the watcher broke into a dead run through green room, wardrobe, and prop spaces. During the chase:

- He threw a wardrobe rack into the hallway, tangling up several party members.
- The party tracked him through a short hallway with four doors; a shadow under the first left door revealed his route.
- The party burst through and followed him through an active backstage room.
- He grabbed a cloak from wardrobe, threw it over himself, and escaped through a side exit.
- Outside, he fled into Castle Ward side streets.
- Elior struck him with Sorcerous Burst after Aladeen inspired him, slowing him.
- The watcher covered a street lamp with the stolen prop cloak, plunging the street into darkness, but the party found the sound of his footsteps.
- Elior hit him again with a stronger Sorcerous Burst, dropping him to hands and knees before he reached a small raised plaza.

At the plaza, the watcher met another man. Aladeen cast Sleep between them, dropping the watcher. The other man grinned and asked if the opera-goers enjoyed the tragedy, saying they had been sitting in the wrong part of the story. Combat began.

## Castle Ward Ambush

The ambush consisted of the watcher, the apparent leader [[People/NPCs/Mr Jones]], and several hired blades or crossbowmen emerging from doors, a stable or outbuilding, and nearby cover. The attackers seemed prepared for a street fight but not prepared for this party's particular nonsense.

Major combat beats:

- Aladeen used Vicious Mockery, Bane, Bardic Inspiration, Sleep, a dagger, and a great deal of movement. He also picked up the giant prop cloak from the lamp.
- Broethuul, wearing formalwear and no armor, used his rapier to devastating effect. He bloodied Mr. Jones before Jones acted, killed him soon after, and used Fallen Puppet to make Jones's corpse smash the original watcher with a mace.
- Davy fought like a phantom rogue should: hiding behind crates and corpses, shooting from shadow, and repeatedly disappearing.
- Jonathan wore full kit to the opera, including armor and morningstar. He used Searing Smite, crushed at least one ambusher, healed Broethuul with Cure Wounds, was later dropped by a critical crossbow shot, then rolled a 20 on his first death save and came back up at 1 hp.
- Elior used Thunderwave with divine glyph imagery, killing one ambusher through forced movement and damage, and used Poison Spray/Sorcerous Burst in close quarters. The poison manifested as runes shifting from gold to red and melting into the target.
- Several scimitar attacks against Jonathan and Elior failed spectacularly; the ambushers were much more effective with crossbows.

Known combat results:

- Mr. Jones died.
- Multiple hired attackers died.
- One original watcher/survivor escaped after using rogue-like movement and disappearing into an open sewer manhole roughly a quarter mile away, with Broethuul pursuing far enough to see the escape route and then returning.
- One remaining mercenary surrendered, throwing down his scimitar and saying he was not dying for "that piece of shit."
- Jonathan survived despite being dropped.

## Prisoner and Loot

The surrendered mercenary said:

- the ambushers were hired for a job;
- it was mercenary work;
- they were told to jump a bunch of dandies when they showed up;
- the employer/leader was "Mr. Jones";
- Jones promised 40 gp per person, payable after the job;
- the mercenary had not been paid yet.

Aladeen slapped him and searched Mr. Jones's body. Loot recovered from Jones:

- 13 gp and 6 sp;
- a simple mace;
- a heavy crossbow, which someone took;
- poor-quality/stinking leather armor;
- [[Objects/Scrap of Note]], revealed to the group.

The note reads, in part, that the Stone is secure, "X" and "Z" each have one eye too many, a third eye no longer rests with Lady B, and asks whether to contact R. The party immediately began speculating that X might be Xanathar, Z might be Zhentarim, and R might be Renaer, but none of that is confirmed.

## End State

- The party is in Castle Ward back streets, near the site of the ambush, after combat.
- One mercenary prisoner is alive and talking.
- Mr. Jones and several attackers are dead.
- One survivor escaped into the sewers.
- Broethuul made it back from the chase route with a successful Survival check.
- The party has the revealed [[Objects/Scrap of Note]] and recovered coins/equipment.
- The opera is still going on behind them, but the transcript stops before any return to the theater, Mirt, Laeral, Watch response, or cleanup.
- The Maxeene mission is assigned but not started.

- [[Factions/Harpers]] became an active party faction track; all five PCs accepted silver harp-and-moon pins.
- [[People/NPCs/Victoro Cassalanter]] and [[People/NPCs/Ammalia Cassalanter]] appeared and offered a loan discussion.
- [[People/NPCs/Laeral Silverhand]] appeared publicly at the opera.
- [[People/NPCs/Remallia Haventree]], [[People/NPCs/Margo Verida]], [[People/NPCs/Zardoz Zord]], [[People/NPCs/Orond Gralhund]], and [[People/NPCs/Yalah Gralhund]] were identified in the audience.

## Clues and Consequences

- The party now has Harper pins and a Harper mission to find Maxeene.
- Cassalanter financing has entered table truth as an available loan lead at Cassalanter Villa.
- [[Objects/Scrap of Note]] is the first explicit table-facing clue naming the Stone and implying faction "eyes."
- A watcher was monitoring Box C and the party before fleeing.
- The ambush appears connected to the watcher and the note, but exact faction ownership remains unknown.
- One survivor escaped into the sewers, potentially alerting someone.
- The surrendered mercenary can still be questioned at the start of the next session.
