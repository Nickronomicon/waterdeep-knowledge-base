# Session 04 - 2026-03-22

Session 4 began mid-morning on Ches 2, 1492 DR, after the long night of Floon's rescue. The party inspected [[Places/Trollskull Manor]], met the [[People/NPCs/Nat|Nat]], [[People/NPCs/Jenks|Jenks]], and [[People/NPCs/Squiddly|Squiddly]], confirmed the manor is likely haunted, began turning the building into a home and future tavern, met several Trollskull Alley neighbors, received a Harper opera invitation through [[People/NPCs/Threestrings]], and ended the evening deciding to answer [[People/NPCs/Vajra Safahr|Vajra Safahr's]] summons to [[Places/Blackstaff Tower]].

## Start State

- The party had returned [[People/NPCs/Floon Blagmaar]] to [[People/NPCs/Volo]] at the [[Places/Yawning Portal]] in the early morning.
- Volo offered the [[Objects/Trollskull Manor Deed]] instead of the promised cash reward.
- Session 4 began the next morning, with the party finding their way to [[Places/Trollskull Alley]] in the North Ward.

## Major Scenes

### First Look At Trollskull Manor

- Trollskull Manor was described as the oldest building on the street, four stories tall with narrow balconies, a jutting turret, five crooked chimneys, uneven shutters, a cracked upper window, and a tired sign creaking above the entry.
- The floor plans included with the deed were treated as potentially aspirational.
- Inside, the front taproom smelled of dust, old wood, stale beer, mildew, and vinegar from split casks.
- The taproom was full of broken chairs, warped tables, tarnished forks and cups, grime, and neglect.

### The Urchins

- Three children burst from behind the bar while playing at defending the place from a "troll skull" intrusion:
  - [[People/NPCs/Nat]], a lanky girl with a wooden sword who communicates through signs;
  - [[People/NPCs/Jenks]], a rounder boy with a toy wand, stuffed owlbear, and mask;
  - [[People/NPCs/Squiddly]], a skinny tiefling boy with an eyepatch and toy bow.
- They had been using the manor as a hangout and partial shelter.
- They described the "troll skull ghost" as an angry presence that lives in the walls, breaks bottles, stomps around, and writes messages.
- The party offered them a place to stay and a possible role helping with the future tavern.
- The children warned that the basement was not a good place to sleep.

### Basement Incident And First Haunting Signs

- The cellar was a serious health hazard full of rot, damp, mold, mildew, fungus, and old beer sludge.
- Elior tried to remediate the cellar with a fire Sorcerous Burst. This ignited mold spores, shook the house, rained plaster, and changed the smell from poisonous to poisonous and burnt.
- The urchins fled upstairs.
- In the taproom dust, fresh writing appeared: "closing time."
- Later, when the party talked seriously about reopening the tavern, a chair slid out as if offering a seat.
- During cleanup, Aladeen saw a rag on the bar briefly wiping dirt away when no one looked directly at it.
- Aladeen and Elior both sensed evidence of a haunting upstairs; Elior concluded it was more likely a naturally occurring haunting than a summoned or arcane construct.
- When Broethuul dismissed the haunting, a bed shifted about a foot.

### Planning A Tavern

- The party discussed reopening the tavern, possibly with a haunted-house theme.
- They considered the guild complications around labor, tavern operation, and repair.
- The urchins offered to help guard the place, act as street-corner callers, keep lookout, and clean, while admitting they knew little about construction.
- The party discussed training Nat and Squiddly in swordplay.
- Aladeen and Broethuul stayed at the manor to clean and manage the children while Elior went for a guild estimate.
- Aladeen improvised a ballad about the party's early adventures while everyone cleaned.

### Emmet Frewn Visits

- [[People/NPCs/Emmet Frewn]] arrived with a cheap bottle dressed up as a neighborly gift.
- Frewn introduced himself as the proprietor of [[Places/Frewn's Brews]], the party's nearest competitor.
- He admitted he had tried to buy Trollskull Manor before Volo outbid him.
- He complained bitterly about guilds and took a pointed interest in the manor's condition, cellar, roof, and repair costs.
- Aladeen visited Frewn's Brews and found it hastily converted from a shop, with two unimpressive taps, a poorly extended bar, few customers, and a roof that showed Frewn's own handiwork.
- The urchins disliked Frewn; Nat described him as a man who counts other people's coins before his own.

### Guild Estimate

- Elior went to the [[Factions/Carpenters', Roofers', and Plaisterers' Guild|Carpenters', Roofers', and Plaisterers' Guild]] to request an inspection.
- Later that afternoon, [[People/NPCs/Sukov]] arrived with two apprentices.
- Sukov identified roof work as the urgent priority, followed by damaged wall sections, exterior weatherproofing, failing plaster, and city sign-off.
- The estimate to make the building structurally fit for trade was 250 dragons total.
- The guild could start with 125 dragons up front, with the remaining 125 needed before opening sign-off.
- The work would take about two tendays, mostly because plaster needed time to set.
- The estimate was explicitly for safety and legal serviceability, not luxury, fashion, or cosmetic restoration.
- Sukov also implied that Frewn's self-installed roof was not guild-approved and had already attracted guild paperwork.

### Corellon's Crown And Fala

- Aladeen visited [[Places/Corellon's Crown]] to find aromatics for the manor's smell.
- [[People/NPCs/Fala Lefaliir]] greeted him warmly as one of Volo's friends and new neighbors.
- Fala provided sage for burning and a lavender, baby's breath, and thistle bundle to hang in the common room.
- Fala gave these supplies freely, saying the whole alley would benefit if Trollskull Manor became grand again.
- Aladeen noticed that Fala also keeps potions on the shelves.

### Room Claims And First Night

- The party decided the house was safe enough to sleep in, though not yet safe enough to serve customers.
- Room assignments were discussed and partly decided at the table:
  - Davy was associated with the attic bedroom in table chatter, though the exact room claim should be confirmed later.
  - Broethuul wanted the attic storage room.
  - Jonathan claimed the den/turret area.
  - Aladeen took a second-floor bedroom and gave the adjacent spare room to the urchins.
- The urchins moved in immediately. Squiddly's belongings were a pillow, a broken-horned tiefling figurine, and a quiver of blunt wooden arrows.
- The library contained damaged books, mostly fiction, plus at least two older Volo titles.

### Threestrings And The Opera Invitation

- Near evening, [[People/NPCs/Threestrings|Matrim "Threestrings" Mereg]] arrived with a better bottle of brandy than Frewn's gift.
- He said [[People/NPCs/Renaer Neverember]] had visited the Yawning Portal the previous night and spoken well of the party.
- Threestrings delivered an origami-like paper bird that unfolded into an invitation.
- The note said Renaer thought the party was a good bet and had bought tickets to the opera tomorrow night at [[Places/Lightsinger Theater]] in the Sea Ward.
- The party was invited to meet [[People/NPCs/Mirt]] at intermission in private box C.
- Formal attire is required for admittance.
- Most of the party recognized that a Mirt with a private opera box was likely the famous Mirt the Moneylender, the old adventurer associated with Durnan.
- Threestrings offered to bring Davy something formal to wear in the morning.

### Vajra's Sending

- After the group split for evening activities, Jonathan received a Sending from [[People/NPCs/Vajra Safahr]], the Blackstaff.
- Jonathan gathered the party and reported that Vajra had summoned him to Blackstaff Tower and told him to bring his friends.
- When Jonathan asked why, the answer he received was essentially: Waterdeep has noticed you.
- The party debated whether to go that night or in the morning. Because the sun had only just gone down and an invitation to Blackstaff Tower is rare, they decided to go immediately.
- Jonathan found Broethuul outside, where Broethuul had been studying roof access and possible future roof sabotage.
- The session ended with the party heading toward Blackstaff Tower.

## NPCs Introduced Or Changed

- [[People/NPCs/Nat]], [[People/NPCs/Jenks]], and [[People/NPCs/Squiddly]]: Trollskull Alley urchins who now have a room in the manor and want to help.
- [[People/NPCs/Lif]]: the manor's ghost is not named in table dialogue yet, but the haunting is confirmed strongly enough to track.
- [[People/NPCs/Emmet Frewn]]: rival tavern owner and failed Trollskull bidder.
- [[People/NPCs/Sukov]]: guild inspector/foreman for the Carpenters', Roofers', and Plaisterers' Guild.
- [[People/NPCs/Fala Lefaliir]]: herbalist neighbor who gave the party aromatics and offered future support.
- [[People/NPCs/Threestrings]]: revealed as an active intermediary for the invitation to meet Mirt.
- [[People/NPCs/Mirt]]: named as the opera contact.
- [[People/NPCs/Vajra Safahr]]: contacted Jonathan through Sending and summoned the party.

## Clues And Information Gained

- Trollskull Manor is very likely haunted by a naturally occurring spirit or similar presence.
- The ghost appears responsive to tavern talk and may approve of reopening.
- Reopening the manor as a tavern requires guild involvement and at least 250 dragons in structural repairs before legal sign-off.
- Frewn is bitter about losing Trollskull Manor and suspiciously interested in the party's repair costs and failure points.
- Corellon's Crown is a friendly local resource with herbs, aromatics, and potions.
- Renaer has connected the party to Mirt through Threestrings.
- Vajra Safahr has noticed the party and wants them at Blackstaff Tower.

## Money, Property, And Obligations

- Structural repair estimate: 250 gp/dragons.
- Required to start guild work: 125 gp/dragons up front.
- Expected timeline: about two tendays for roof, walls, weatherproofing, failing plaster, and sign-off.
- Fala gifted the aromatics; no cost recorded.
- Frewn gifted a cheap bottle of wine.
- Threestrings brought brandy and an opera invitation.
- The deed is in the party's hands and the manor is treated as their new home, but the transcript does not show a magistrate scene; exact legal transfer status remains worth confirming.

## End State

- Date: evening of Ches 2, 1492 DR.
- Location: departing Trollskull Alley for Blackstaff Tower.
- The party has begun occupying Trollskull Manor.
- The urchins are sleeping in their new room.
- The manor needs substantial repair before opening as a tavern.
- The party has an opera invitation for the following night, likely Ches 3.
- Vajra Safahr has summoned the party to Blackstaff Tower.
