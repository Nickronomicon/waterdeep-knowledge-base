# Session 12 - 2026-07-09

Session 12 began on the morning of Ches 6, 1492 DR, after [[People/NPCs/Lif|Lif's]] seance at [[Places/Trollskull Manor]]. It resolved several Trollskull setup obligations, advanced the renovation through two time skips, sent the party on a [[Factions/Gray Hands]] mission to investigate a bronze dragon in [[Places/Deepwater Harbor]], and ended on the morning of Ches 16 with Trollskull almost ready to open.

## Starting State

- The party woke in Trollskull Manor after the seance, with Lif's "welcome" still behind the bar.
- The [[Objects/Cassalanter Loan]] was negotiated but unsigned, with funds expected at midday.
- [[People/NPCs/Corvin Underbough]] and [[People/NPCs/Miri Underbough]] were due back for the hire decision after their rat-interrupted trial dinner.
- Lif had asked the party to tell [[People/NPCs/Fala Lefaliir]] that he sends his regards.
- The manor still had a temporarily blocked [[Places/Trollskull Smuggler's Passage]] to the sewers, a bad cellar smell, and major repairs pending.

## Fala And Lif

At sunrise, the party went to [[Places/Corellon's Crown]] and told Fala that Lif had spoken during the seance and sent his regards.

- Fala was visibly shaken and saddened, confirming that Lif had been a good former owner who wanted very little beyond opening Trollskull as a tavern.
- Fala repeated the local understanding that Lif died crushed under a cask or failed support while moving ale in the cellar, with no foul play suspected at the time.
- When asked about strengthening Lif, Fala said they would need to research what form of strengthening the party meant.
- Fala later helped the renovation with herbs for the cellar smell and left a note behind the bar: "Tell Lif I remember."

## Missing Urchins

The party realized [[People/NPCs/Nat]], [[People/NPCs/Jenks]], and [[People/NPCs/Squiddly]] had not been seen since the previous day.

- Fala had seen or heard that the children went out "on adventure" armed with their growing collection of practice and emergency weapons.
- Aladeen traced neighborhood rumors toward the Market but did not find them there.
- The children returned later, scratched and bruised but alive, immediately noticing the Cassalanter gold chest.
- The details of the children's adventure remain unknown.

## Underboughs Hired

The Underboughs returned in the morning and were formally hired.

- Corvin asked for 2 gp per tenday for both himself and Miri once the tavern opened, but offered to work for 1 gp 5 sp per tenday during pre-opening work.
- Aladeen judged the requested rate as fair, even low once the tavern was actually operating.
- The party hired Corvin and Miri as Trollskull's kitchen staff.
- Corvin was enthusiastic and immediately returned to the kitchen with Miri.

## Cassalanter Loan Signed

At midday on Ches 6, [[People/NPCs/Willifort Crowelle]] arrived at Trollskull Manor with two porters and a locked chest of Cassalanter gold.

- Willifort presented documents on behalf of [[People/NPCs/Victoro Cassalanter|Victoro]] and [[People/NPCs/Ammalia Cassalanter]].
- The table-confirmed terms remained: 2,000 gp principal, no interest until opening, 40 gp per tenday for the first four months if the principal remains unpaid, then 60 gp per tenday afterward.
- Because the party had no formal company or party name able to hold the debt, all five owners signed individually.
- Elior reviewed the contract and found it straightforward.
- The chest contained 2,000 gp in paper-wrapped rolls.
- The exact post-guild-payment remaining balance is not captured in the transcript and should be confirmed from table bookkeeping.

## Renovation Montage

The session then skipped forward several days as paid work transformed Trollskull.

- Guild laborers patched walls, plaster, windows, and exterior damage.
- [[People/NPCs/Tally Fellbranch]] and apprentices brought furniture and beams.
- [[People/NPCs/Embric]] and [[People/NPCs/Avi]] installed new kitchen fittings while debating whether the old fittings were merely unsafe or actively malicious.
- Fala's herbs improved the cellar smell.
- Lif continued helping: doors latched, mugs stood upright, dust gathered into piles, and drinks were spared from dust.
- Corvin tested tavern staples while Miri inventoried what was usable, edible, drinkable, or hopelessly moldy.

By just before midday on Ches 12, the manor was visibly becoming a business instead of a ruin.

## Bronze Below

On Ches 12, [[People/NPCs/Vajra Safahr]] contacted Jonathan by Sending. A bronze dragon had been seen in Deepwater Harbor. The party was to investigate, learn its intentions, and report to [[Places/Drowned Lantern]] before dusk. Vajra made clear the assignment was not to kill the dragon.

At the Drowned Lantern, the party met [[People/NPCs/Laraelra Harsard|Laraelra "Elra" Harsard]], Vajra's contact, who supplied five potions of water breathing and warned the party not to confuse a friendly dragon with a safe dragon.

## Drowned Lantern Fight

Before the party could enter the harbor, a Xanathar-linked salvage crew tried to take the potions and pursue the dragon's supposed hoard.

- Aladeen used Suggestion on the salvage boss, sending him away before he could become the center of the fight.
- Two dock bruisers, three cutpurses, and a gazer fought the party around the Drowned Lantern and its private dock.
- A cutpurse stole Jonathan's potion, but Jonathan killed him and recovered it.
- Another cutpurse stole Broethuul's alchemist's supplies after mistaking them for a potion; Broethuul later recovered them.
- Davy killed the gazer at close range after Aladeen badly wounded it with a shortbow shot.
- One cutpurse escaped into the harbor; the salvage boss also left under magical compulsion.
- One dead cutpurse carried a crude copied Xanathar symbol, supporting the link to the Guild.
- Elra did not intervene but said the mess was exactly why the Blackstaff had not involved the Watch.

Loot recovered included two scimitars, two light crossbows, a mace, a heavy crossbow, light armor or work clothes, and a small amount of coin. The exact final party accounting is uncertain.

## Zelifarn

The party drank the water-breathing potions and entered [[Places/Deepwater Harbor]], where they found [[People/NPCs/Zelifarn]], a young bronze dragon living near an old wreck.

- Zelifarn was curious, theatrical, and treasure-minded, but not hostile to Waterdeep.
- He had not been attacking ships and seemed more likely to linger, collect odd salvage, ask questions, and eventually get bored than to become an immediate threat.
- Aladeen gave him a silver piece for his collection.
- The party proposed friendship and occasional visits rather than treating him as an enemy or recruit.
- Zelifarn disliked talk of mind flayers and had no interest in illithid business.

## The Amberleaf Dispatch

Zelifarn noticed that [[People/PCs/Broethuul]] smelled like a "wrong-water door" and said another door-smelling air-thing had recently approached the harbor, briefly passed through a door, and left a leaf for Broethuul.

- Zelifarn gave Broethuul [[Objects/The Amberleaf Dispatch]], an autumn leaf bearing a private message.
- The party saw the delivery and understood that the leaf was magical or significant, but Broethuul did not read the message aloud during the session.
- In DM-facing truth, the handout connects Broethuul, the Starfern claim, the Stone/Eye, the broken Crommor horn, the Cassalanter-adjacent "House of Coin and Smile," and the possibility that restored memory could name Broethuul regent by right.

## Report To Elra

After leaving the harbor, the party reported back to Elra.

- They told her Zelifarn was not hostile and did not appear to be a city threat.
- Elra judged that curiosity is not a crime and that the dragon could remain under observation.
- She gave the party five spare [[Objects/Force Grey Water Breathing Potions]] as a backup resource.
- Vajra may contact them again for future Gray Hands work.

## Final Time Skip

The session skipped forward again to the morning of Ches 16, 1492 DR.

- Trollskull was nearly restored: exterior painted, bar sanded, hearth and flue working, kitchen functioning, cellar smell improved.
- Lif began communicating through a chalkboard, renewing "Welcome" every morning.
- When asked whether he can leave the manor, Lif circled "N" and turned it into a frowny face: he cannot leave.
- The tavern still needs its final public name and signboard.
- Across the alley, [[Places/Frewn's Brews]] posted a fresh sign advertising its coming grand opening with "Real drinks, living staff, no ghosts."
- The session ended three days before the party's intended opening window.

## End State

- It is the morning of Ches 16, 1492 DR.
- The Cassalanter loan is signed and the 2,000 gp funds entered play.
- Corvin and Miri Underbough are hired as kitchen staff.
- Fala has received Lif's message and is researching how he might be strengthened or better understood.
- Trollskull Manor is close to opening but still lacks a final name/sign.
- Lif is confirmed unable to leave the manor.
- The party completed a Gray Hands harbor mission, identified Zelifarn as non-hostile, and received five spare water-breathing potions.
- Broethuul privately received the revealed Amberleaf Dispatch.
- Frewn is escalating the tavern rivalry with a public anti-ghost advertisement.

## Open Questions

- What was the urchins' Market adventure, and did it create any follow-up consequences?
- How much of the Cassalanter gold remains after initial guild payments and purchases?
- What will the party name the tavern, and what sign will Tally carve?
- How will the party handle Lif as visible/known staff if the tavern markets its haunting?
- Will Broethuul share the Amberleaf Dispatch with the party, and if so how much?
- Where did the door-smelling messenger come from, and can the harbor threshold be used again?
- Will the escaped cutpurse, magically dismissed salvage boss, or Xanathar-linked crew report the harbor incident?
- How will Frewn's anti-ghost positioning affect opening week?

- Tissina Khyret appeared in prep as part of the loan delivery, but only Willifort Crowelle and porters appeared in transcript truth.
