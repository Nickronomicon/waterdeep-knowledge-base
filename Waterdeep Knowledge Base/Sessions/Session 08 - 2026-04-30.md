# Session 08 - 2026-04-30

Session 8 began after the Castle Ward ambush outside [[Places/Lightsinger Theater]]. The party did not return to the opera, report immediately to [[People/NPCs/Mirt]], or seek the Watch. Instead, they resolved the prisoner as best they could, returned to [[Places/Trollskull Manor]], slept, handled morning household chaos, and then pursued the [[People/NPCs/Maxeene]] mission at [[Places/Thomril & Sons Cartage]].

The in-world date is inferred as late Ches 3 into the morning of Ches 4, 1492 DR: session 7 happened on the evening of Ches 3, and session 8 includes a long rest before the Maxeene visit.

## Starting State

- The party had accepted [[Factions/Harpers]] pins from Mirt.
- Mirt's first Harper job was still pending: find [[People/NPCs/Maxeene]] and learn what she overheard from Zhentarim operatives.
- [[People/NPCs/Mr Jones]] and several hired ambushers were dead.
- One survivor had fled into the sewers.
- One surrendered hired thug remained in the party's hands.
- The party held [[Objects/Scrap of Note]], recovered from Mr. Jones.

## Castle Ward Aftermath

- The surrendered mercenary was never given a prepared name; the table nicknamed him [[People/NPCs/Thugby]].
- He claimed to be local hired muscle who worked bars and was hired to jump "a bunch of dandies."
- The party tried to turn him into a future warning system or saboteur if he was hired against them again.
- Jonathan intimidated him effectively enough that Thugby appeared to understand the danger of crossing the party again.
- The transcript re-reads the recovered note as: "Stone secure. Blinded. X has one eye too many now, as does Z. Third eye no longer rests with Lady B. Keeping watch in the meantime. Contact R?"
- The party added a map note for Thomril & Sons outside the North Gate/near Undercliff.

## Return to Trollskull

- Elior wanted to press on to Maxeene immediately, but the rest of the party persuaded him to sleep because Jonathan was at 1 hp and resources were low.
- The party returned to Trollskull Manor very late. [[People/NPCs/Nat]], [[People/NPCs/Jenks]], and [[People/NPCs/Squiddly]] were asleep.
- The heavy crossbow taken from Mr. Jones was assigned to Jonathan, who is proficient with it.
- The simple mace from the ambush loot was left in the urchins' room with their existing emergency weapons.
- Session 8 table accounting appears to treat the Castle Ward ambush coin as about 17 gp, though session 7 transcript ingest recorded 13 gp and 6 sp. Treat the exact coin count as a small table-accounting uncertainty unless Foundry inventory settles it.

## Morning at Trollskull

- The urchins woke the party with loud thumps: Jenks had found the mace and was swinging it at the ruined bed frame while Nat and Squiddly cheered.
- The party did not take the weapon away, but suggested the children should learn to use it properly.
- The children were given three copper for breakfast, bought a loaf, ate some of it, and returned late with an apology from Nat and bread still being chewed.
- Elior prepared obsessively for the Maxeene interview, practicing questions and presentation.
- Davy worked on darkening the extremely loud opera outfit using soot, charcoal, lantern oil, and forgery/tool work; the project went unusually well, muting the outfit into a darker gray.
- Jonathan prepared by sitting on his bed and staring at the wall in full readiness.
- Nat and Squiddly tried to light the hearth when told to make a fire. Squiddly could produce a spark, but the chimney/flue was blocked or stuck, filling the room with smoke.
- Jonathan ran for water and doused the hearth, declaring the problem fixed. Trollskull still needs chimney work before the hearth is useful.

## Broxley Fairkettle

[[People/NPCs/Broxley Fairkettle]] arrived at Trollskull Manor as a representative of the [[Factions/Fellowship of Innkeepers]].

- He had heard that the party took over Trollskull Manor and was excited that the old tavern might reopen.
- He was visibly crestfallen when he saw the building's actual condition.
- He asked about the haunting. The party framed the matter as friendly supernatural or highborn residents.
- He explained that the Fellowship helps with rules, hours, guidelines, expectations, and guild membership for operating an inn or tavern.
- Dues were estimated at about 10 gp per tenday as part of regular business expenses.
- He warned that other guild members would dislike a non-guild tavern operating in the city.
- He said [[Places/Frewn's Brews]] is a guild member, though late on dues, and implied Frewn's clientele should not be Trollskull Manor's target clientele.
- When moneylenders came up, Broxley suggested the [[Factions/Cassalanters]] as probably the biggest relevant moneylenders.

## Maxeene at Thomril & Sons

The party traveled to [[Places/Thomril & Sons Cartage]], a cartage stable outside the North Gate/near Undercliff. The place had an attached shop, working stable space, drays and sledges, a dung heap, and the smell of a business kept outside the walls for practical reasons.

- The stablemaster, likely [[People/NPCs/Whelan Thomril]] from prep, was confused by the request for "Maxine/Maxeene" but pointed the party to a large draft mare.
- Maxeene is a talking draft horse and Harper contact.
- She waited until the stablemaster could not hear, confirmed the party's Harper connection, and welcomed them to the Harpers.
- She gathers intelligence because people talk near animals and assume they cannot understand.

Maxeene's Zhentarim information:

- Two days earlier, she gave a ride to a sun elf and a half-orc/orc bodyguard, later identified by table context as [[People/NPCs/Davil Starsong]] and [[People/NPCs/Yagra Stonefist]].
- She picked them up at an intersection she did not remember and dropped them at [[Places/Yawning Portal]].
- They discussed hiring enough spies to root out [[Factions/Xanathar Guild]] hideouts in the city.
- They did not sound like the Watch or do-gooders; they sounded like rivals or newcomers making a mark.
- The party connected this to the [[Factions/Zhentarim]] / Black Network.

Maxeene's Stone-related information:

- A rock gnome had hired her twice and talked about a "stone," apparently a precious or significant stone.
- On one ride, the gnome hired her to follow behind some lord's carriage.
- On another, he had her follow a well-dressed red-haired man on foot, repeatedly trying to slow the dray.
- This was at least a week earlier.
- Maxeene did not know the gnome's name.
- She did not recognize the "Third Eye," "Lady B," or other terms from [[Objects/Scrap of Note]].

## The Thomril Stable Fire

While the party was interviewing Maxeene, Davy and Aladeen heard people at the back of the stable. One voice asked whether the spy worked there; another said they were going to burn it down anyway.

- Aladeen attempted to redirect suspicion with a lie about Mirt having a spy that was a giant rat.
- Broethuul got Maxeene out of her stall and warned the stablemaster that the barn was on fire.
- Jonathan charged toward the danger with Divine Favor active.
- Davy found and filled a bucket, then hid in Maxeene's stall.
- Elior climbed to the hayloft for a high angle.

The attackers included several thugs, [[People/NPCs/Thugby]], a fire-obsessed gnome caster, and at least one poison-weapon operative with a flying-snake tattoo.

Major combat beats:

- Elior dropped Thunderwave from the hayloft onto attackers below, then was shot unconscious by crossbow fire.
- Davy risked an opportunity attack, took a mace blow, reached Elior, and stabilized him with a DC 10 Medicine check.
- Davy then dragged Elior down from the burning loft.
- Jonathan used Divine Smite, Searing Smite, Divine Favor, and later Lay on Hands, discovering or remembering in play that Lay on Hands was available.
- Aladeen created the "spy rat" illusion and shouted that the real spy rat had escaped, drawing attention and buying confusion.
- Aladeen used Healing Word on Jonathan and later healed Maxeene, which probably saved her from being killed by the poison-weapon attacker.
- Broethuul recognized Thugby among the attackers. Thugby recognized the party, froze, failed to commit to the fight, and fled.
- Another attacker shot at Thugby as he ran, but missed.
- The stablemaster/owner was shot in the leg, then killed by a mace blow while trying to confront the attackers.
- The gnome caster spread fire with Fire Bolt, Flaming Sphere, and Aganazzar's Scorcher, igniting more of the stable.
- Jonathan killed the gnome with radiant damage; the gnome's last confusion was that the light did not burn.
- Maxeene fought for herself, kicking and eventually killing the poison-weapon attacker who tried to kill her.

## Loot and Evidence

From the poison-weapon attacker Maxeene killed:

- 11 gp and 8 sp.
- A short sword coated in a foul, sticky poison.
- A hand crossbow.
- Four poisoned crossbow bolts dealing extra poison damage on a hit.
- A flying-snake tattoo curled up his arm, pointing toward Zhentarim affiliation.

From the gnome pyromancer:

- Two daggers.
- A spear.
- A fire agate crystal, likely a spellcasting focus.
- 18 gp and 3 sp.
- Assorted fire-starting oddments, including string, a broken glass marble, and old bird's nest/tinder.

The party discussed putting more weapons into the urchins' improvised armory. Davy took the poisoned crossbow bolts because he can use a light crossbow.

## End State

- The party obtained the information Mirt wanted: Davil Starsong and Yagra Stonefist are apparently Zhentarim-linked and are trying to plant spies to locate Xanathar Guild hideouts.
- Maxeene survived, knows Trollskull Manor's location, and may stop by if the party has hauling work for her.
- The stablemaster/owner of Thomril & Sons is dead, likely Whelan Thomril from prep, though the transcript did not capture his name at the table.
- The stable is burning badly; by session end the building is not resolved.
- Thugby escaped alive again and now knows the party recognizes him.
- At least one attacker with a flying-snake tattoo died, strengthening the Zhentarim connection to the attack.
- The party has not yet reported the Maxeene information to Mirt, Remallia, Vajra, or the Watch.
- The session ended at roughly 10 p.m. real time immediately after combat and looting.
