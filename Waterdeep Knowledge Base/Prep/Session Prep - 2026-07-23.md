# Session Prep - 2026-07-23

Session 13 is the Trollskull grand opening session. The target arc is: finish the last opening decisions, make the tavern feel like a living social hub, let the players show off their roles, interrupt the party with one compact street-war problem if time permits, and end with the opening's own result, fallout, or quiet after-hours image. Fireball is not part of this session; it is the Ches 22 pressure beat for the following session.

## Target End State

- The tavern has a public name and sign.
- The party has hired or assigned enough staff to plausibly operate.
- Lif's role in the tavern is either public, concealed, or deliberately theatrical.
- Frewn's rivalry remains a background nuisance: rumors, shills, complaints, and plausible deniability.
- The grand opening stands on its own: final business result, reputation shift, Frewn rumor fallout, or a quiet closing beat with Lif.
- Fireball remains scheduled for Ches 22, 1492 DR, likely in the next session.

## Session Boundary

- Do not run Fireball in session 13.
- Treat Ches 19 as the grand opening day.
- Use Ches 20-21 as connective space if needed: first business days, rumors, Frewn fallout, faction messages, or Dalakhar/Renaer setup.
- Fireball is fixed for Ches 22, 1492 DR, and should launch the next major pressure sequence.

- Is Lif visible, hidden, or framed as part of the attraction?
- Did the party invite anyone specific?

## Very Short Story Beat

Run this before the grand opening.

Tally arrives with the finished signboard covered in a canvas cloth. He has left the name blank or unfinished until the party says the final words. Fala brings a small bundle of herbs and a simple note for Lif: "I remember." Lif writes "WELCOME" on the chalkboard, pauses, wipes it away, and writes "OPEN?"

Ask for the tavern name and the sign image. Once the party decides, Tally carves or paints the final detail in front of them, Fala pins a small green sprig behind the bar, and Lif turns every mug in the taproom handle-out at the same time.

Optional future setup: Renaer can mention that agents from his father's old life still surface from time to time, but do not put Dalakhar on a direct approach to Trollskull tonight. Save the concrete meeting pressure for Ches 22.

## Grand Opening Resolution Track

Keep a visible private tally:

- normal success: +1
- normal failure: -1
- critical success: +2
- critical failure: -2
- excellent roleplay, clever spend, or good use of NPC: +1
- public panic, visible violence inside the tavern, or successful Frewn rumor: -1 to -3

Use the tally as the modifier to the two grand-opening result rolls from the outline. Also use it fictionally: positive means good buzz, negative means expenses, complaints, or guild pressure.

## Between-Phase Interruption: Street War Spillover

Best placement: after phase 1, while the tavern resets for evening. This gives the players a compact action beat that is clearly not about Trollskull's business, but still yanks them away from their own party for distracting city nonsense.

### Hook

During the reset between crowds, a wounded runner bolts into Trollskull Alley, glances at the tavern's open door, and decides the crowd is cover. Two Xanathar Guild toughs and a hovering gazer round the corner seconds later. The runner is Zhentarim-adjacent, but he is not here for the party; he is here because the alley has bodies, doors, noise, and witnesses.

If the party ignores it, the chase crashes through the edge of the opening crowd. One guest is hurt, the Watch gets called, and the opening tally takes -2.

### Battlefield

Use the alley outside, not the taproom. Make the cleanest solution feel like "get this away from our opening." Features:

- crowded street edge with bystanders;
- open tavern door with curious guests behind it;
- stacked ale casks and sign-delivery crates as half cover;
- sewer grate or side passage as an escape route;
- one lantern that can be knocked over but should not become a major fire;
- Frewn watching from across the alley, absolutely ready to benefit from any public panic.

### Enemies

For five level-3 PCs:

- 1 Xanathar Guild bruiser using thug stats; flees at half HP or when Watch whistles sound.
- 2 Xanathar Guild cutters using bandit stats.
- 1 gazer scout, preferably loud and recognizable from the campaign's Xanathar texture.
- 1 wounded Zhent runner using spy or bandit stats, but he avoids fighting unless cornered.

If the party is fresh and crushing it, add one more bandit from a side alley on round 2. If time is tight, have Watch whistles sound at the start of round 3 and make every hostile prioritize escape.

### Enemy Goals

- Xanathar side: grab the runner's message tube, punish him publicly, and disappear into the sewer.
- Zhent runner: survive, keep the tube, and use the crowd as cover.
- Everyone except the gazer: avoid being arrested.
- Gazer: loudly identify at least one PC, then flee if hurt.

### Checks During Combat

- DC 13 Perception before initiative: spot the runner palming a message tube or one cutter moving toward the tavern door.
- DC 13 Persuasion, Intimidation, or Performance: get guests back inside or away from the fight without panic.
- DC 14 Athletics: overturn a crate, shove a cask, or block the sewer escape.
- DC 14 Insight: neither side planned this around Trollskull; the opening is just useful cover.
- DC 15 Investigation or Sleight of Hand: secure the message tube before the runner or Xanathar side escapes with it.

### Rewards And Consequences

- Clean win: +1 or +2 opening tally; the owners protected the alley and got back to business.
- Messy win: +0; guests murmur, Frewn smirks, but the night survives.
- Hostiles crash through the tavern or hurt a guest: -2 opening tally and Watch pressure increases.
- Message tube recovered: give [[Objects/Zhent Runner Message Tube]]. It confirms that the street war is still searching for a rock gnome, a stone, or old Neverember channels, but stays brief and partial.
- Runner escapes with the tube: later rumor says the Zhents lost someone near Trollskull and the Xanathar Guild thinks the party interfered.

## Future Fireball Setup: Ches 22

Do not run this in session 13 unless the session plan changes at the table. The opening should get to be a complete episode before Chapter 3 detonates into the campaign.

Soft setup only:

- Trollskull's public opening makes it a plausible place for Dalakhar to seek Renaer on Ches 22.
- Renaer can mention that an old Neverember agent may be looking for him soon, but not that the meeting is tonight.
- A rock gnome can be glimpsed in distant city rumor, faction chatter, or a later message; avoid having him reach Trollskull on Ches 19.
- End session 13 on opening-night consequences: the first till count, Lif's chalkboard, a review from Broxley, Frewn watching from across the alley, or the exhausted household finally closing the door.

## Flexible Fallbacks

- If hiring/planning runs long: skip phase 1 events down to Sign Reveal, Broxley Inspection, and Lif Trick.
- If the street fight runs long: make phase 2 a quick montage of three guests and end with opening-night results.
- If the table does not need combat: run the street-war spillover as a three-check skill challenge to protect guests, block escape, and grab or lose the message tube.
- If the party fixates on Frewn: let the planted complaints become the closing social conflict; do not make him the architect of real violence yet. Fireball still waits until Ches 22.
- If they leave Trollskull entirely: use an invited guest, urchin alarm, or Lif's chalkboard to pull them back; if not, resolve what the opening becomes in their absence.
- If they refuse a public opening: make it a soft opening for neighbors and faction guests only; Trollskull can still become known enough for Dalakhar to choose it on Ches 22.

## Secrets And Clues To Seed

- The tavern is now public enough for Dalakhar to choose as a Ches 22 meeting point.
- Someone in the underworld knows Trollskull's cellar/sewer access matters.
- Frewn is willing to use shills, rumors, bad-faith complaints, and ghost panic, but his rivalry should stay petty and plausibly deniable.
- The Zhentarim-Xanathar street war can intrude on Trollskull without being about Trollskull.
- Cassalanter patronage is becoming publicly visible.
- Harpers and Gray Hands both now see Trollskull as useful, but Harper attention should stay deniable through Threestrings, Remallia's people, or a silver raven.
- Elra should reinforce that Vajra trusts the party's competence after two successful jobs and is thinking forward about how to use them when Waterdeep needs deniable, capable agents.
- Thugby can become a recurring low-level chaos contact if he survives his own instincts.
- The party's choices around Lif will shape Trollskull's reputation.
