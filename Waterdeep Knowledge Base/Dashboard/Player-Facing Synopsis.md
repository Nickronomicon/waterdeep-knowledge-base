# Dragon Heist Synopsis

Player-facing campaign synopsis suitable for posting between sessions. Keep this in the same broad style as the original Discord synopsis: narrative, lightly wry, specific about what the party experienced, and free of DM-only secrets.

## Session 1

Relaxing in their favorite watering hole, the Yawning Portal, our party was listening as Volothamp Geddarm wrapped up a rousing tale when a brawl broke out. Yagra Stonefist, a large half-orc mercenary, was accosted by a bald thug with eye tattoos named Krentz. Yagra put Krentz on the floor, Krentz's friends put Yagra on the floor, and Durnan put an end to the whole thing before anyone could make the sort of decision that gets memorialized in court documents.

Before the tavern could settle back into its usual rhythm of drinking, gambling, and pretending not to notice the giant hole to the Undermountain, something began dragging itself up from the well. A troll hauled itself into the taproom with stirges clinging to it like the world's worst accessories. Durnan met the troll with steel, while the party dealt with the hungry little bloodsuckers. Jonathan helped bring oil and fire into the equation, and Aladeen found time, from behind the bar, to mock the troll with enough commitment that one has to respect the craft.

Once the troll was burning and the stirges were no longer anyone's immediate problem, Volo approached the party with a job. His friend Floon Blagmaar had gone missing after a night of drinking, last seen at the Skewered Dragon in the Dock Ward. Floon would be hard to miss: handsome, red-blond, and wearing a truly unreasonable unicorn medallion on a chain of blue pearls. Volo paid 10 gold apiece up front and promised another 100 each if the party found him. With fresh coin, road flagons, and perhaps an excess of confidence, our heroes headed into the night.

## Session 2

With road flagons in hand and Volo's money in their purses, the party set out from the Yawning Portal toward the Dock Ward. The city did not feel festive. On the way, they found a side street blocked by the City Watch, half a dozen bodies cooling on the cobblestones, and a few bloody men being hauled off in irons. Elior asked whether any of the dead wore a giant unicorn medallion with blue pearls. They did not. But the bodies told their own story: black flying-snake tattoos on some, eye-like markings on others. Whatever feud was brewing between the Zhentarim and the Xanathar Guild had spilled out into the streets.

Elior attempted what might generously be called civic lubrication with a gold coin. One Watch officer seemed briefly open to the possibilities of private enterprise. Another was not. Jonathan was also, unsurprisingly, not. The party moved on before the Scene became an Event.

At the Skewered Dragon, the mood was sour, suspicious, and probably flammable. Dockworkers drank cheap beer with the practiced discipline of people who had no intention of becoming involved in someone else's problem. After a little money, a little rum, and some careful conversation, the party learned that Floon had indeed been there the night before. He had been drunk, loud, rich, and very much not in his natural habitat. Another well-dressed man joined him, and the two left together shortly before Midnight Bell. Four or five rough-looking men followed them out soon after. At least one of those men may have been wearing a Zhentarim mark.

Meanwhile, Broethuul decided the evening needed a little texture and attempted to start a bar fight by stealing coins from a gambling table and blaming someone else. This nearly worked. Aladeen vanished from the blast radius, Davy melted into the background, Elior used a gust of magic to send coins skittering across the tavern, and Jonathan managed to talk the room down through sheer glowing sincerity. The result was not so much a brawl as a brief, ugly coin storm. Nobody died, which counts as diplomacy in some wards.

The party then crossed the street to Old Xoblob Shop, a curiosity shop so purple it seemed less decorated than infected. There they met Xoblob, a deep gnome with lavender smoke, purple goods, purple enthusiasm, and an unsettling interest in Broethuul's eyes. Xoblob confirmed that two well-dressed men had been jumped outside his shop by five rough men in dark cloaks and black leather armor. He pointed the party north. Elior bought a purple tankard cozy, because apparently the night still had room for personal growth.

Outside Xoblob's, Davy and Broethuul spotted something bright blue in the gutter: the broken remains of Floon's unicorn necklace. The medallion was there, but many of the pearls were missing. A little searching revealed a trail of blue pearls leading north toward Candle Lane, where the street lamps had mostly been smashed and the warehouses stood dark and boarded.

The trail ended at a warehouse marked with a black winged snake. In the yard lay a dead flying snake with an arrow through it, which is never a sign that a building is full of excellent choices. The party debated stealth, performance, and a traveling-entertainer approach before Jonathan elected to kick in the doors and demand that evildoers show themselves.

The evildoers showed themselves with arrows.

Four kenku opened fire from the dark. Jonathan went down almost immediately, proving that justice may be bright but is not arrowproof. Aladeen brought him back with Healing Word. Davy fired from cover. Broethuul shot through the fence and dropped a kenku. Elior contributed both ice and a frankly concerning yellow-gold magical theme. Jonathan, once vertical again, returned the favor by healing Elior after Elior hit the floor. By the end, several kenku were dead, one had fled into the street, and one remained inside, terrified, disarmed, and repeating fragments of things it had heard.

From that panicked kenku, the party learned enough to make the night worse. "Xanathar sends his regards." "Tie up the pretty boy in the back room." "Follow the yellow signs in the sewers." "No time to loot the place. Get him to the boss."

Searching the warehouse revealed bodies from both sides of the gang war, old pickled-herring stink, and signs that the place was being used more as a hideout than an honest business. In a storage room under the stairs, Davy found someone breathing beneath a tarp. Elior pulled it away to reveal not Floon, but Renaer Neverember, son of the former Open Lord.

Renaer was exhausted, filthy, and extremely relieved to see people who were not currently trying to kidnap him. He explained that he had come to retrieve Floon after hearing his friend was in a state from drinking with Volo. Zhentarim thugs grabbed both men, apparently mistaking one for the other, and questioned Renaer about his father and the rumored cache of embezzled dragons. Then another group, likely Xanathar's people, attacked the warehouse, killed the Zhents, searched the place, and took Floon.

Upstairs, the party found a chair with rope bonds, Renaer's smashed locket, and a strange sheet of parchment that gave off magical vibes without being polite enough to explain itself. Elior mended the locket and returned it to Renaer, though whatever had been hidden inside its secret compartment was gone.

And so our heroes ended the night in a warehouse full of bodies, with Renaer Neverember at their side, Floon still missing, a lead pointing toward yellow signs in the sewers, and the sound of hobnailed boots approaching from outside. A perfect time, in other words, to consider whether Volo's promised payment was perhaps a little light.
