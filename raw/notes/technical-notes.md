# Technical Notes

## How we play
This campaign is remote. We get on discord every thursday night at 5 or 6pm Pacific time. I have a foundry instance hosted [here](https://nickronomicon.io/) for our tabletop, and character sheets were originally created in a [dndbeyond campaign](https://www.dndbeyond.com/campaigns/7332962), and they're only updated from Foundry on a change in level. Otherwise, Foundry character sheets are the canonical source.

## Session structure
We aim for 4 hour sessions once per week, we occaisonally run over 4 hours and often have to skip weeks due to other obligations. The session videos are recorded using OBS, the audio is ripped to mp3 with ffmpeg, then transcribed with Whisper and diarized with pyannote.

> **N.B. whisper/pyannote is not great at diarization!**
> it has a hard time consistently distinguishing speakers, often attributing spoken statements to the wrong person. The transcript speaker IDs are unstable between sessions, but also cannot be relied on for accuracy even within a single transcript!

We're a group of friends, so off-topic table-talk is quite common. We aim for a 10 minute break halfway through the session, and it usually takes some time to get the topic of conversation back on the game.


## Homebrew
Almost everything is an official Wizards of the Coast or Critical Role licensed product, but in the case of Broethuul's class specialization, we took the Critical Role Blood Hunter class and homebrewed an "Order of the Faeslayer" specialization.

So far, this is our only major homebrew.

## Discord setup
Every player has their own public #{character-name} channel where they can discuss character related stuff with the group. They also have a a #{character-name}-private channel that is only shared with the GM. This is used for table whispers and the like.
There is a #campaign channel for meta-campaign discussion between sessions. There is also a #handouts channel where I drop either actual handout pdfs/jpegs or just drop snippets of text in.
The #synopsis channel was supposed to be updated after every session, but due to time constraints, it rarely gets an update. I intend to change that going forward.

Finally, there is the #Session voice channel where we play.

## Documents
The majority of our document sharing happens via Google docs, especially anything that requires more length or formatting than discord supports, as well as any private handouts I need to share with a player. Links to those docs (once shared) are usually in the #handouts channel or on the individual #{character-name}-private channels.


## DM notetaking
Almost all of my notes are in service of session prep, so they tend to be aspirational and contain things that were never used or had to change on the fly. They almost never accuratly reflect the real state of the campaign. I do a terrible job of post-session note taking, instead relying on my memory and the recordings for whatever I need. Again, this is something I hope to fix going forward.
