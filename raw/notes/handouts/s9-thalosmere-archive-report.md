# Thalosmere Archive Report
## Subject: Lady Remallia Haventree, Widow Ulbrinter
**Archive Classification:** Civic-Noble, Watchful Interest<br>
**Compiled For:** Elior Thalosmere<br>
**Reliability:** Uneven. Public records are clean; social records are degraded by subject’s prolonged withdrawal from public life.<br>
**Archivist’s Note:** The following should be treated as a profile of tendencies, not proof of allegiance.

---

## I. Identifying Particulars
**Name:** Lady Remallia Haventree<br>
**Common Address:** Lady Haventree; “Remi” in older correspondence from intimate circles<br>
**Residence:** House Ulbrinter, Delzorin Street, North Ward<br>
**Status:** Widow of Lord Arthagast Ulbrinter<br>
**Ancestry:** Sun Elf<br>
**Public Standing:** High, though diminished in visibility<br>
**Current Public Pattern:** Reclusive for approximately five years, with irregular exceptions for selective social functions, patronage obligations, and private receptions.

House Ulbrinter remains well-kept, though quieter than it was in Lord Arthagast’s lifetime. Staff turnover appears low. Deliveries are regular but not extravagant. The household gives the impression of disciplined restraint rather than decline.

---

## II. Public Reputation
Lady Haventree is remembered favorably among older civic families, senior guild factors, certain temple stewards, and retired adventuring circles. Her reputation rests on three qualities:

First, discretion. She does not use grief as theater and has avoided the more predatory forms of noble mourning politics.

Second, hospitality. Prior to her withdrawal, House Ulbrinter was known for gatherings that mixed nobles, artists, retired adventurers, and reform-minded civic figures. The guest lists were eclectic but rarely vulgar.

Third, restraint. No credible record places her in debt, scandal, legal censure, or coercive guild dealings. That absence should not be overvalued; skilled operators leave clean records. Still, in Waterdeep, five clean years are not nothing.

---

## III. The Five-Year Withdrawal
The death of Lord Arthagast Ulbrinter appears to have marked a decisive contraction of Lady Haventree’s public life. She ceased regular attendance at several seasonal gatherings, reduced open invitations, and delegated charitable correspondence through intermediaries.

The conventional explanation is grief.

The archive accepts grief as plausible but incomplete.

Observed inconsistencies:
* House Ulbrinter did not become dormant. It became selective.
* Certain old contacts continued to visit, though not always through formal entrances.
* Charitable disbursements persisted, often routed through minor agents or “anonymous friends.”
* Several visitors known for adventuring, intelligence work, or civic trouble-shooting appear in proximity records, but not frequently enough to establish conspiracy.

**Conclusion:** Lady Haventree withdrew from society, not from activity.

---

## IV. Known and Suspected Associations
**Mirt:** Publicly plausible. Social overlap between Mirt and the Ulbrinter circle predates the current unrest. Mirt’s presence alone proves little; the man is everywhere he should not be and half the places he should.

**Renaer Neverember:** Plausible acquaintance. Renaer moves among noble reformists, adventurers, and politically inconvenient friends. His association with Lady Haventree would be unsurprising.

**Laeral Silverhand:** No reliable direct pattern. Any noble of Lady Haventree’s rank may correspond with the Open Lord’s circle indirectly.
Artists, musicians, and theater patrons: Historically strong overlap. House Ulbrinter’s older gatherings favored culture as a civilizing instrument rather than mere ornament.

**Adventurers:** Repeated but irregular contact. This is notable. Lady Haventree appears more willing than most nobles to treat competent outsiders as people rather than disposable tools.

---
## V. Political Disposition
Lady Haventree’s documented sympathies lean toward the preservation of Waterdeep’s civic order without enthusiasm for tyranny, demagoguery, or noble impunity. She appears to favor quiet correction over public denunciation.

She has no known loyalty to the Zhentarim, Xanathar Guild, Asmodean circles, Luskan interests, or Neverember loyalists.

This does not mean she lacks factional commitments, just that the archive cannot prove them.

Her probable values:
* Stability without authoritarianism
* Protection of the vulnerable, especially when official mechanisms fail
* Preference for information over force
* Willingness to use deniable intermediaries
* Low tolerance for predators operating behind wealth or title

---

## VI. On the Matter of Good Character
The archive dislikes moral summaries, they are imprecise.

That said: Lady Haventree’s pattern is more consistent with principled concealment than corrupt concealment.

Predatory nobles usually leave pressure marks: coerced servants, frightened creditors, vanished rivals, sudden legal favors, desperate petitioners, or unexplained enrichment. House Ulbrinter shows little of this.

Instead, the pattern suggests a woman with resources, grief, discipline, and private obligations. She may be dangerous, but she is unlikely to be cruel.

Provisional assessment: **Lady Remallia Haventree is probably a good person, if that phrase is permitted to include secrecy, manipulation, and selective truth-telling in service of civic ends.**

---

## VII. Current Relevance
Lady Haventree has recently begun permitting more movement around House Ulbrinter. A forthcoming or recent social event at the villa should not be dismissed as a return to ordinary noble hospitality. After five years of careful quiet, any public-facing gathering is likely purposeful.

Possible explanations:
1. Re-entry into noble society after mourning.
2. A controlled test of current political weather.
3. A screening event for agents, allies, threats, or all three.
4. A lure.

The fourth possibility should not be assumed, but it should also not be ignored.

---

## VIII. Guidance for Approach
If you meet Lady Haventree, do not flatter her grief. Do not pretend ignorance of House Ulbrinter’s former significance. Do not pry crudely into her late husband.

Speak plainly. She is likely practiced at hearing lies from charming people.

Recommended posture: respectful, observant, and morally serious.

Watch the room more than the hostess. A recluse who hosts selectively is not only receiving guests; she is measuring who arrives, who avoids the invitation, who watches whom, and who fails to notice they are being watched.

Final archive note: **Lady Haventree may be trusted to want Waterdeep preserved. She should not yet be trusted to tell you how she intends to preserve it.**
