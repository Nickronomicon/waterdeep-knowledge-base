# The Amberleaf Dispatch
*A Whisper Folded in Autumn*

*Delivered on a leaf that does not rot. Read only beneath falling light.*

---

## To the Ninth Petal Cast Beyond the Hedge
You were not lost.

You were planted.

When the Axe Wind cut through Starfern bough and our halls burned in amber silence, you were carried not into exile, but into soil too stony for our rivals’ roots.

You fell through a thin place where the sea drinks memory. The mortals call it a harbor. We call it a seam.

The seam remembers you.

---

## Of the Broken Horn and the Stone That Eats Names
In the city of stacked stone and salt, a maiden carried a horn that did not sing. It carried warning instead.

Before it could speak, a Hand of Iron brushed it from her grasp.

Stone kissed brass.

The sound rang not in air, but in contract.

You felt it, whether you admit it or not.

That was not clumsiness.

That was a ledger closing.

---

## The Buried Eye
Mortals once feared their own remembering.

So they fashioned an Eye and fed it truths they could not bear to hold.

They named it a stone.

They pretend it sleeps.

It does not sleep.

It swallows.

It swallowed gold.

It swallowed treachery.

It swallowed us.

Long ago, when Autumn still walked the harbor openly, Starfern signed a tide-compact beneath red leaves and ship-masts. Mortals wished passage through our thresholds; we wished order in their crossings.

The pact was witnessed by the Eye.

Then came fear.

Mortals feared owing a season to any but themselves. They fed the pact to the Eye and called it prudence.

The memory vanished.

With it went our claim.

Without memory, a throne is rumor.

With memory, it is law.

---

## Of Iron Roots in Forgotten Soil
Where memory thins, iron grows.

The House of Coin and Smile, the one that binds its fortunes in ash and blood, has rooted itself in soil once marked Autumn’s.

Infernal vines thrive where old wards are forgotten.

The broken horn was not an accident. It was pruning.

They did not wish the girl to speak of what the Eye had hidden.

They do not know the Eye remembers.

They do not know we remember the Eye.

---

## Of You, Ninth Petal
You drift in mortal revels like a leaf in eddying tide, laughing at gravity.

Yet the harbor seam hums when you pass.

You are Starfern.

The pact was not erased from blood. Only from tongue.

If the Eye is turned outward, if its swallowed season is restored, the harbor’s thresholds would name you.

Not duke by story.

Regent by right.

Autumn does not forget.

Autumn waits.
