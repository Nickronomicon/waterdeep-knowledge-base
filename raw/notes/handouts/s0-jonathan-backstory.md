# JONATHAN USMAN SONNY THEODORE ICARUS CHARLES EAGLESHIELD
*A Private Family Account*

You were born beneath bells.

It is said that when your first cry rang through Eagleshield Manor, the chapel lamps flared brighter, though no oil was added. The midwife swore she saw light behind your eyes before you opened them. Your mother wept. Your father knelt.

You were the first Aasimar ever born into the house of Eagleshield.

Your uncle, Narrovan Eagleshield, called it a blessing. Others called it destiny.

From childhood you were told two things:

1. You are chosen.
2. You must prove worthy of it.


You were taught blade and lance before poetry.

Law before leisure.

Justice before comfort.

The city was your inheritance, but righteousness was your burden.

## The Proposed Alliance

When you were nineteen, your uncle began speaking more frequently with House Crommor.
The Crommors were merchants turned nobles: ambitious, sharp, well-connected in trade and shipping. There were murmurs that a formal alliance between Eagleshield and Crommor would strengthen both houses: military honor joined to mercantile wealth.

Nothing was ever signed.

Nothing was ever announced.

But at a private dinner, you were introduced to Anastasia Crommor.

She was younger than you by several years. Too young, perhaps, for anything immediate, but noble alliances are patient things.

You remember:

* She spoke plainly.
* She did not simper.
* She asked you whether justice always required punishment.
* You did not have an answer ready.

You found that irritating and intriguing.

There were two more dinners. Then three. You walked in a garden once, escorted and supervised.

No betrothal was declared, but it was understood.

You did not love her, not yet. But you respected her.

And you thought (perhaps) you could have grown into something steady and honorable together.

## The Fall of House Crommor

Then came the scandal.

Rumors. Financial irregularities.

Whispers of impropriety.

A private matter that became public.

House Crommor’s standing collapsed with stunning speed.

Anastasia disappeared from public life.

No explanation was given to you beyond:
> “The alliance is no longer under consideration.”

You were told it was for the best.

You were told the Crommors had proven unworthy.

You were told not to involve yourself.

But something about the speed of it unsettled you.

It did not feel like justice.

It felt… arranged.
