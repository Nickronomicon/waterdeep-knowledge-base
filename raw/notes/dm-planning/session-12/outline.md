# Session 12 Run Sheet

## 1. Cold open: morning after the séance, 5 minutes

Start in the taproom. The grime behind the bar still says **WELCOME** from Lif. That is important because the séance revealed two immediately useful things: Lif knows of a mostly blocked smuggler’s passage to the sewers, and Fala Lefaliir may know more about strengthening or understanding him. 

Read/paraphrase:

> Morning light finds Trollskull Manor in a strange mood. The place still smells of old damp, dead rats, and last night’s cooking, but the word **WELCOME** remains written in clean strokes behind the bar. The manor no longer feels empty. It feels expectant.

Then ask only:

> “Before the loan signing at midday, is there one thing each of you wants to handle?”

Hard cap this. One action each. No shopping expedition.

Likely actions:

* Visit Fala.
* Inspect the secret/smuggler’s passage.
* Hire or reject the Underboughs.
* Clean up rat corpses.
* Decide where the incoming gold will be stored.

If they inspect the passage early, let them find it mostly blocked and foul, but do **not** trigger the combat yet. Tell them it will need workers/tools.

## 2. Loan signing: Willifort Crowelle, 10–15 minutes

This should be crisp and faintly insulting.

### Scene frame

At midday, a black coach stops outside Trollskull Manor. Willifort Crowelle enters with a locked coffer, a leather document case, and two Cassalanter house guards or porters. Tissina Khyret stands half a step behind him and says nothing.

Willifort should not act villainous. He should act like a senior servant forced to deal with tavern amateurs.

### Willifort’s posture

* Never says “Master Cassalanter” when “Lord Cassalanter” will do.
* Looks at the walls before sitting.
* Brushes dust from the table before laying down the documents.
* Calls the manor “promising” in the exact tone one uses for a sickly foal.
* Treats Jonathan’s family backing as useful collateral, not nobility-level equality.
* Treats Aladeen as a potential future entertainment expense.
* Watches Davy a fraction too long, but says nothing.
* Is polite to Elior if the noble bearing is obvious, but not deferential.
* Gives Broethuul the widest possible professional berth.

### Read/paraphrase

> “Lord and Lady Cassalanter extend their congratulations,” Crowelle says, placing the contract on the least-stained table. “They are pleased to invest in civic renewal, neighborhood stability, and the productive ambitions of persons of demonstrated usefulness.”

Then he looks around.

> “Naturally, some properties require more imagination than others.”

### Signing mechanics

Do not invite legal analysis unless they initiate it. Say:

> “The terms are as agreed: repayment begins after opening, forty dragons per tenday for the first four months, sixty dragons per tenday thereafter. All deeded owners sign, unless you have established a legal company or named fellowship empowered to hold debt.”

That ties to the table joke from the transcript: the party discussed needing a party name or legal structure, and you reminded them that in 1492 DR liability mostly rests on connections. 

If they start spiraling into Waterdeep LLC law, Willifort shuts it down:

> “Waterdeep has guilds, magistrates, contracts, creditors, and prisons. It does not require you to invent a new branch of jurisprudence before lunch.”

### Underbough beat

When the coffer opens, show the money.

> The coffer does not glitter. It *presses* against the room. Stacked dragons in neat paper-wrapped rolls. Heavy enough that two porters shift their grip when Willifort gestures them forward.

Corvin, if hired or still nearby:

> “That is… rather more gold than I expected to see in a place with no locks on half the doors.”

Miri adds, quieter:

> “And a cellar door that just grew teeth last night.”

This is enough. Do not have them steal anything yet. Let the players become suspicious, but give no proof.

## 3. Fast renovation montage, 10 minutes

After the loan is signed, time skip **six to eight days**. I would not skip all the way to opening night yet unless the table is impatient. You want one combat and one visible progress beat first.

Use a montage with three decisions, not a spreadsheet.

### Ask these three questions

1. **What is the tavern’s public name?**
   They can keep Trollskull Manor, but make them choose.

2. **What is the first thing people see when they enter?**
   Bar, hearth, stage, trophy wall, shrine, menu board, haunted gimmick, etc.

3. **What job do you personally take during renovation?**
   Examples: negotiating guilds, cleaning, guarding the money, designing signage, testing food, chasing rats, watching the workers, entertaining neighbors.

Then narrate results.

### Renovation beats

Use these images:

* Tally Fellbranch’s apprentices haul in new beams and mutter about “load-bearing optimism.”
* Embric and Avi argue over whether the kitchen fittings are merely unsafe or “actively malicious.”
* Fala sends over herbs to deal with the cellar smell, plus a note: **“Tell Lif I remember.”**
* Lif starts helping: doors latch themselves, mugs appear upright, dust sweeps itself into accusing little piles.
* Corvin begins testing tavern staples; Miri starts inventorying anything edible, drinkable, or not yet moldy.

The source material expects the players to personalize, refurnish, rebuild, and reopen the tavern; Lif becomes accommodating once they genuinely renovate with reopening in mind.  The Alexandrian also emphasizes letting the players design the place so it becomes “their place,” not just a long-rest building.

# 5. End state for Session 12

End with the tavern nearly ready, not fully open unless pacing demands it.

Good closing image:

> By the end of the tenday, Trollskull Manor no longer looks abandoned. The sign is being painted. The bar has been sanded smooth. The hearth draws clean. The kitchen smells like onions, yeast, and hot iron instead of mildew.
>
> Behind the bar, where the word **WELCOME** had appeared in grime, Lif has rewritten it in the dust every morning no matter how often someone wipes it away.

Then drop one hook:

> Outside, a boy from the Waterdeep Wazoo is asking what the tavern will be called for the opening announcement.

Or, if you want a sharper ending:

> Across the alley, someone has pasted a fresh notice to the wall:
> **FREWN’S BREWS — GRAND OPENING SOON. REAL DRINKS. LIVING STAFF. NO GHOSTS.**

That gives them a clean rival target without derailing into the Grand Game yet.

## Practical pacing

For a 3–4 hour session:

| Segment                     | Target Time |
| --------------------------- | ----------: |
| Morning one-action check-in |      10 min |
| Loan signing                |      15 min |
| Hire/Underbough decision    |      10 min |
| Renovation montage          |      15 min |
| Combat setup                |      10 min |
| Combat                      |   60–90 min |
| Aftermath decisions         |      25 min |
| Closing montage/hook        |      10 min |

The key discipline: **no price lists at the table.** Every time someone asks a business-detail question, answer with one of three buckets: “covered by the loan,” “requires one extra favor/scene,” or “not possible before opening.”
