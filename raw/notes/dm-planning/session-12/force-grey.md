# Force Grey Mission: “Bronze Below”

## Why Vajra picks them

Vajra has heard about the party through Renaer, Volo, and general North Ward gossip. The Remix notes that Vajra controls Force Grey and uses it for problems requiring specialist adventurers; it also specifically suggests she may try to recruit the PCs into Force Grey. 

This mission is a good test because it requires restraint. Vajra is not looking for dragon-slayers. She is looking for people who can investigate a dangerous creature without immediately escalating.

Also, the rules of Waterdeep support the harbor setup: Ahghairon’s dragonward blocks dragons from entering the city and sewers, but it does **not** extend into the harbor. 

---

# Session Placement

Use this after the loan signing. Keep your planned Willifort scene intact: Willifort delivers the documents and money, Tissina silently watches, Willifort is snobbish, and Corvin comments on where they intend to keep so much gold. 

Then, as renovation begins, a Force Grey runner arrives.

## Hook

Vajra sends Johnathan a message via sending spell:

> “A bronze dragon has taken up residence in Deepwater Harbor. It startled the sailors but hasn’t hurt anyone. Confront the dragon and learn its intentions.”

Follow up:


> You are not being sent to kill it.
>
> Report to the Drowned Lantern in the Deepwater docks before dusk. Potions will be provided.

At the dock, Vajra herself does not need to appear. Use **Laraelra “Elra” Harsard**, Vajra’s right hand, if you want a lower-status contact who can be blunt with them.

Elra’s briefing:

> “Drink these before you go in. They’ll keep you breathing. They won’t keep you from being stupid. A bronze dragon that young can still kill you with one angry breath, so do not mistake ‘friendly’ for ‘safe.’ Ask questions. Listen. Come back.”

Each PC gets one **potion of water breathing**.

# Dock Combat: “The Eye on the Pier”

I would put the combat **on the way to the dragon**, not after. You said you want to get to combat fairly quickly, and this lets the mission open with action before shifting into the underwater social scene.

## Premise

A Xanathar-linked salvage crew has heard rumors of a dragon nosing around a wreck in the harbor. They assume “dragon” means “hoard,” and they have been shaking down dockhands, cutting mooring lines, and threatening sailors to learn where the creature lairs.

They see the party arrive with Force Grey potions and decide these new adventurers know where the treasure is.

## Location

A slick stretch of Dock Ward pier just before dusk.

Use these features:

* **Narrow pier:** 10 feet wide. Forced movement can knock someone into the harbor.
* **Cargo stacks:** Half cover. Can be shoved over with DC 13 Athletics, creating difficult terrain.
* **Mooring ropes:** DC 12 Dex save if a creature dashes through loose lines or falls prone.
* **Crane hook:** A PC can use an action to swing a suspended cargo net. DC 13 Dex save or the target is knocked prone.
* **Harbor water:** Falling in is not lethal, but it changes the fight. Anyone who already drank the potion has a major advantage.

## Enemy Roster for five level 3 PCs

Use:

* **1 Xanathar salvage boss** — spy stat block.
* **2 dock bruisers** — thug stat block.
* **3 cutpurses** — bandit stat block.
* **1 gazer** — hovering spotter, not a committed combatant.

This should be a firm but not overwhelming encounter. If your party is depleted or missing a player, remove one thug. If they are fresh and dangerous, give the salvage boss 10 extra hp and a smoke bomb.

## Enemy Tactics

Round 1:

* Cutpurses throw knives and try to flank.
* Thugs use shove attempts to knock PCs into the harbor.
* Salvage boss stays behind cover and uses a hand crossbow.
* Gazer hovers under the pier, using eye rays through gaps in the planks.

Round 2:

* One cutpurse tries to grab the potion satchel or a character’s pack.
* One thug cuts a mooring rope, causing a small boat to swing into the pier. Creatures in a 10-foot line make DC 12 Dex saves or take 1d6 bludgeoning and fall prone.
* Gazer tries to flee if damaged.

Round 3:

* If the boss is bloodied, he yells: “Fine! Let the dragon eat you!”
* The crew starts retreating toward Mistshore or into a waiting skiff.

## What They Want

The crew is not there to fight to the death. They want:

* The location of the dragon.
* Any treasure recovered from the wreck.
* Proof to sell to Xanathar’s people that a dragon is in the harbor.

## Clues

On the salvage boss:

> “Bronze wyrmling or young. Harbor wreck south of the black buoy. Curious. Greedy. Not city-warded while it stays in the water. X pays for proof. More for scales. Most for leverage.”

On a cutpurse:

> A crude sketch of a round-eyed symbol: Xanathar’s mark, but drawn by someone who has clearly never met Xanathar and is copying gang-sign secondhand.

If the gazer escapes, Xanathar now knows the PCs made contact with the harbor dragon. That is useful later, but it does not need to pay off immediately.

---

# Transition to the Dive

After the fight, Elra or the Force Grey boatman is irritated but not surprised.

> “That,” Elra says, looking at the bodies, “is why the Blackstaff did not ask the Watch to handle this. Too many eyes. Too many bribes. Too many people who hear ‘dragon’ and think ‘coin purse.’ Drink up.”

Then the party descends.

# Zelifarn Scene

## Underwater Set

The harbor drops into green-black gloom. Sunlight becomes bands, then stains, then disappears. The wreck sits half-buried in silt, barnacle-covered and canted sideways.

Details to use:

* The ship’s nameplate is obscured by coral and mussels.
* Schools of silver fish move through the broken hull.
* A bronze tail occasionally flashes in the murk.
* Coins, cutlery, broken glass, and brass fittings have been arranged into little “collections” around the wreck.
* Zelifarn has made a crude gallery of “interesting surface things.”

## Zelifarn’s Personality

Zelifarn is friendly, vain, curious, and treasure-motivated. He does not understand why sailors object to being startled.

Voice:

> “Oh! Air-things. But not drowning. Good. Much easier. Are you officials? Adventurers? Thieves? I like thieves. They bring things worth stealing.”

He wants:

* Coins.
* Stories.
* Shiny trinkets.
* News about Waterdeep.
* Someone to explain why sailors scream when a dragon surfaces beneath their boats.

He does **not** want:

* To attack Waterdeep.
* To live in the city.
* To fight Force Grey.
* To admit he is lonely.

## Key Questions and Answers

**Why are you here?**

> “The harbor is interesting. Everything falls into it eventually.”

**Are you guarding treasure?**

> “Guarding? No. Sorting. Categorizing. Appreciating.”

**Have you hurt anyone?**

> “No. One sailor fainted. That was not my fault. He was already very faintable.”

**Will you leave?**

> “Why? Is the harbor yours? Did you bring a deed?”

**What would make you stop startling ships?**
He can be persuaded to avoid active traffic lanes if the PCs offer him something interesting: a story, a trinket, a map, a promise of future salvage rights, or an introduction to someone knowledgeable.

## Mission Check

Use the published DC:

* **DC 13 Wisdom (Insight):** Zelifarn poses no danger to Waterdeep.
* If nobody succeeds, they can still report that he was friendly and nonviolent, but they cannot be certain.

Give advantage if a PC offers treasure or engages him respectfully instead of interrogating him.

# Broethuul Handout Moment

Trigger this when Broethuul first descends near the wreck or when Zelifarn circles him.

Zelifarn notices something:

> The bronze dragon stops circling. For the first time, his playful motion goes still. His eyes narrow at Broethuul.
>
> “You smell like a door,” he says. “Not a ship-door. Not a house-door. A wrong-water door.”
>
> "Another door smelling air-thing tried to approach our waters recently. They left back through the door after a few moments, but this remained. I think it's for you.

Then give Broethuul The amberleaf Dispatch.

## If Broethuul shares it

Zelifarn says:

> “Oh. That is much better than treasure.”

He has felt the “wrong-water” flicker around the wreck but does not understand it. He has been collecting objects from the area because “things touched by it shine differently.”

Possible clue object for Broethuul:

* A piece of green-black glass.
* A barnacle-crusted silver leaf.
* A coin with a starfern pattern corroded into it.
* A scrap of fabric that never gets wet.

Do not explain it yet. Just let it sit.

# If the Party Attacks Zelifarn

Do not roll a normal fight unless the players insist after multiple warnings.

Use a three-step escalation:

1. **First hostile action:** Zelifarn dodges, laughs, and moves out of reach.

   > “Oh! Is this how surface people say hello badly?”

2. **Second hostile action:** He uses terrain, not breath: tail-sweep silt cloud, wreck collapse, or a thunderous roar through the water. Everyone makes a DC 13 Con save or is deafened until end of next turn.

3. **Third hostile action:** He leaves, fast. If they somehow pursue and continue, then they chose the massacre.

This keeps the scene from becoming a CR 8 execution while preserving the truth: picking a fight with a young bronze dragon at level 3 is suicidal.

---

# Resolution

When they report back, Vajra or Elra wants three things:

1. Is Zelifarn hostile?
2. Is anyone else aware of him?
3. Did the PCs escalate?

Good report:

> “Then he stays,” Elra says. “For now. Harbor’s not the city, and curiosity isn’t a crime.”

Reward:

* Gray Hand/Force Grey member gains **1 renown**.
* Vajra lets the party keep any unused water breathing potions only if they handled the matter discreetly.
* Force Grey now considers them reliable enough for future work.

Bad report, but no disaster:

* They still get credit if they learned the dragon is not an immediate threat.
* Elra makes it clear they are being evaluated for judgment, not just bravery.

If they attacked Zelifarn:

* No renown.
* Vajra is displeased.
* Zelifarn becomes a recurring annoyed NPC.
