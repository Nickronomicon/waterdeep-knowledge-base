## DM Run Sheet: Back Half Session

**Start state:** The party has just escaped the Dock Ward warehouse after killing the grell. They recovered:

1. **Cargo chit for three ironbound crates**
2. **One broken-open empty ironbound crate**
3. **Note fragment: “Loose it, then leave”**

They already know:

* something with a floating eye was around Trollskull last night
* someone may have tried to get in upstairs
* Threestrings is due this afternoon with formalwear for Davy
* they are expected at the opera tonight to meet Mirt at intermission 

This run sheet is built to turn that into a full session without another large combat.

---

# Session spine

## Act 1 — Afternoon at Trollskull

* decompress after Dock Ward
* let Trollskull react
* surface the break-in pressure
* point the clues toward one short follow-up

## Act 2 — Clue follow-up mini-adventure

* trace the cargo chit / crate trail
* find a frightened surviving handler or broker
* learn the grell was a panic asset meant to distract the Watch

## Act 3 — Late afternoon opera prep

* Threestrings arrives with clothes
* brief social/personal scene
* one more sign they’re under observation

## Act 4 — Opera and Mirt

* social scan before intermission
* Mirt meeting
* post-intermission spy complication or Trollskull message

# ACT 1 — Trollskull Reset

## Scene goal

Make the world react to the warehouse fight and give the players one clear next step.

## Boxed opener

> Trollskull Alley feels too calm after Dock Ward. The cul-de-sac is washed in late-afternoon light, warm and almost domestic, but there is a tension under it now—curtains moving, doors opening half a handspan and closing again, the quiet certainty that rumor got here before you did.
>
> By the time you reach the manor, the street already knows you have been somewhere dangerous.

## Who is present

Use these three:

* **Fala Lefaliir** from Corellon’s Crown 
* **Tally Fellbranch** from the Bent Nail as your other Trollskull neighbor 
* **Nat, Jenks, and Squiddly** as the local chorus

This gives you:

* Fala for calm, perceptive concern
* Tally for practical groundedness
* the urchins for continuity from last night

## NPC beats

### Fala

Fala notices physical and magical aftermath.
Use:

> “That is not ordinary blood, ordinary fear, or ordinary posture. Which one was it?”

Fala can:

* offer water / herbal wash
* identify slime, ichor, or monster residue as aberrant/unsettling
* quietly ask whether last night’s floating eye and today’s Dock Ward trouble might be connected

### Tally

Tally brings practical neighborhood concern.
Use:

> “I’m not asking out of gossip. If something followed you back, I’d rather know before it starts taking an interest in my shutters.”

Tally can:

* mention seeing an unfamiliar cart or laborers earlier
* point out someone was definitely on a nearby roof last night
* suggest that “ironbound crate” sounds like a shipping problem, not a random street gang problem

### Urchins

Let them be excited but useful.
They repeat:

* “There really was a floaty eye.”
* “It looked in the upstairs window.”
* “Somebody tried the latch, I swear.”
* “Then it zipped off when we yelled.”

## If the party checks the upstairs intrusion now

They find signs of a **search, not a burglary**:

* latch tampered with
* dust disturbed near papers, not valuables
* a room or storage spot looked through
* nothing obvious stolen

### Checks

* **Perception DC 12:** latch or sill disturbed
* **Investigation DC 13:** intruder was looking for information
* **Insight DC 12:** this fits last night’s warning

## Why this matters

It tells the party the warehouse incident and Trollskull attention are part of the same pattern: they are now interesting.

---

## Triggering the clue follow-up

Do not make them guess too hard.

### Best prompt

Fala or Tally reacts to the cargo chit.

If you want Fala:

> “Three ironbound crates sounds expensive, deliberate, and documented. Creatures like that do not simply drift into warehouses by accident.”

If you want Tally:

> “Crates like that get ordered, delivered, and paid for. Somebody in this city handled the wood, iron, cartage, or receipt.”

## What the clue points to

The cargo chit leads to a **small chandlery and shipping broker near Fishgut Court** that handles odd cargo discreetly.

This is your mini-adventure site.

# ACT 2 — Clue Follow-Up Mini-Adventure

## Premise

The cargo chit points to **Rassal’s Chandlery**, a shabby broker front. The real office upstairs was used by a low-level handler involved in moving the grell crate. By the time the PCs arrive, the place is being abandoned.

## Time budget

45–60 minutes

## Scene structure

1. approach and social read
2. search the premises
3. confrontation with surviving handler or lookout
4. recovery of one good lead before the opera

---

## Scene 2A — Exterior

## Boxed read-aloud

> The address on the chit leads to a narrow shop wedged between a cooper and a net-mender: tar, rope, lamp oil, and cheap hardware in the window, nothing memorable enough to invite a second glance.
>
> Which, of course, is exactly what makes it memorable now. The front sign hangs slightly crooked. The door is unlocked. Inside, no bell rings.

## What they notice

* front room looks legitimate but thinly stocked
* papers missing from where they should be
* someone left in a hurry
* faint medicinal smell upstairs

### Checks

* **Perception DC 12:** recent hurried departure
* **Investigation DC 13:** front room is a cover; real work was done elsewhere
* **Insight DC 12:** whoever left did not have time to fully clean the scene

---

## Scene 2B — Upstairs room

The upstairs room is the meat of the scene.

## Boxed read-aloud

> Upstairs, the air turns stale and close. One room has been half-stripped in haste: blanket rolled but not tied, drawer hanging open, papers burned in a basin that still smells faintly of scorched ink. Beside the bed sits a crate pry-bar flecked with dark residue.
>
> Someone is still here.

## Occupant

Use **one surviving handler**, wounded, frightened, and trying to decide whether to bolt, bargain, or lie.

### Handler profile

* low-level Xanathar runner
* not a fanatic
* knows enough to be useful, not enough to blow the campaign open
* terrified of being left alive after talking

### Quick stats: Surviving Handler

* AC 12
* HP 14
* Dagger +4 to hit, 1d4+2
* Deception +4
* Stealth +4
* Morale: surrenders or tries to flee, does not stand and fight unless cornered

## Handler opening line options

If caught:

> “I didn’t loose it. I just moved the crate.”

If confronted hard:

> “You kill me, fine. You leave me, I’m dead anyway.”

If they come in gently:

> “If you’re not Watch, then maybe I still have a chance.”

---

## What the handler knows

Give 3–4, no more.

### Core facts

* The grell was transported in one of the ironbound crates.
* It was meant to be **loosed in public and abandoned**.
* “Loose it, then leave” was standard instruction because the point was panic, not control.
* The disturbance was meant to pull the Watch/Guard toward Dock Ward and away from something else.
* The handler does **not** know the full larger operation.

This aligns with the published use of Xanathar-linked monster incidents as city distractions. 

### Names / phrases he may know

Use one or two:

* “Noska”
* “back underground”
* “fish lane split”
* “don’t stay to watch”
* “not the only crate this month”

### Good line from the handler

> “It wasn’t about feeding it. It was about making people scream where the right people would hear.”

---

## What they can recover in the room

Pick 2–3.

### Evidence 1: partial ledger scrap

A few lines survive the burn:

* rope
* chain
* three ironbound crates
* hired cart
* paid in silver, no guild marking

### Evidence 2: route notation

A scrap that matches the style of the earlier sewer clue:

> fish lane / split / down / eye mark

### Evidence 3: contact token

A cheap brass eye token, same style as before

### Evidence 4: torn secondary note

Not enough to reveal too much, but enough to suggest pattern:

> “…if bells sound, leave the rest below…”

---

## Skill paths

If the party questions the handler:

* **Persuasion DC 12:** he admits he only moved the crate
* **Intimidation DC 13:** he gives names/phrases, but may embellish
* **Insight DC 12:** he is telling the truth about being low-level and scared
* **Medicine DC 11:** stabilizing or treating him improves his cooperation

## Decision point

What do they do with him?
That is the scene’s real choice.

Possible outcomes:

* leave him tied for Vajra’s people
* escort him to a safer place
* scare him into disappearing
* hand him to a trusted intermediary
* disastrously hand him to the Watch

Each is playable. None need derail tonight.

---

## Optional brief action beat

If you need 5–10 minutes more energy, add one **lookout on the roof** who tries to flee when he sees the PCs enter.

### Quick stats: Roof Lookout

* AC 12
* HP 9
* Speed 30
* shortbow +3 to hit, 1d6+1
* Goal: run, not duel

This can become a one-minute chase across adjoining roofs, not a major fight.

# ACT 3 — Late Afternoon Reset / Opera Prep

## Goal

Transition from Dock Ward grime to opera elegance while paying off Threestrings.

## Boxed read-aloud

> By the time you return to Trollskull, the alley has settled into that hour when businesses wind down and windows begin to glow. The city seems to be pretending it has already forgotten the ugliness of the day.
>
> It has not.

## Threestrings arrival

Threestrings promised attire for Davy, so make that entrance count.

### Boxed read-aloud

> Threestrings arrives like a man who considers timing a kind of music. He has a bundle over one shoulder, a second tucked under one arm, and the expression of someone who expected trouble and is pleased to find he was right.

### Threestrings tone

* gently amused
* more perceptive than he lets on
* does not pry directly, but notices everything

### Useful line

> “Opera forgives late arrivals more readily than it forgives the wrong cut of coat. Let us solve the important crisis first.”

## What he brought

* appropriate attire for Davy
* maybe one or two stopgap pieces for anyone else underdressed
* enough taste to make the party plausible, not noble

## Add one social beat here

Choose one:

* Fala assists with cleaning stains or calming nerves
* Tally offers a practical loaned cane, clasp, or polished wooden pin
* the urchins are scandalized by formalwear
* Jonathan ends up responsible for the group’s etiquette on very short notice

### Best combination

Use **Fala + Tally**:

* Fala handles presentation and subtle reassurance
* Tally contributes a concrete accessory or fix, grounding the community feel

This keeps Trollskull Alley alive and communal. 

---

## Add one fresh sign of surveillance

Before they leave for the opera, include one more reminder:

* a figure across the alley lowers a newspaper too late
* a raven on the roofline
* a chalk eye-mark appears near the drain again
* Lif silently closes the upstairs shutters before anyone asks

No scene. Just pressure.

# ACT 4 — Opera and Mirt

The Harper invitation is still the anchor: *The Fall of Tiamat*, Mirt at intermission. 

## Scene 4A — Arrival at Lightsinger Theater

## Boxed read-aloud

> The Lightsinger Theater glows in the evening like a jeweled lantern. Velvet, gilt, crystal, and candlelight turn even the air expensive. Here, people wear rank as carefully as perfume.
>
> Your invitations carry you past the public crowd toward private boxes, where the city’s powerful come to admire tragedy from a safe height.

## Pre-intermission observations

Give them 3 things to notice before Mirt.

Pick 3:

1. **Renaer** is present, relaxed but watchful
2. a servant spends too long looking toward the party’s box
3. one guest is clearly pretending to be less important than they are
4. a noble crest one PC recognizes
5. a familiar face from Dock Ward among staff or service corridors

### Best choice for this session

Include **a familiar face from the chandlery thread**:

* the clerk
* the cart driver
* the witness
* someone who absolutely should not be here

This helps the whole session feel connected.

---

## Scene 4B — Intermission with Mirt

## Boxed read-aloud

> The first act ends in thunderous applause. Servants move with wine and folded napkins through a hum of silk and speculation. In the next private box sits a man who looks equal parts indulgent grandfather, retired predator, and delighted conspirator.
>
> Mirt raises his glass. “My dear friends,” he says, “Waterdeep is never more honest than when everyone in the room is pretending.”

## Mirt’s purpose here

Not just recruitment. Interpretation.

He should help the players understand what they already learned:

* the grell was not the point
* public chaos is often camouflage
* the interesting question is who used the panic, and for what

### Mirt line to use

> “A floating horror in Dock Ward is dramatic. Which is why I assume it was meant to be noticed. The more interesting matter is what was supposed to pass unnoticed while everyone was looking at it.”

### Harper pitch, lightly

The Harpers value:

* truth
* discretion
* seeing the pattern behind the spectacle

Mirt is not trying to outbid Vajra. He is offering a different toolset.

### If they share the clues

He reacts strongly to:

* “Loose it, then leave”
* multiple crates
* evidence of distraction rather than attack

### What Mirt can provide

Pick one:

* confirms this pattern is not isolated
* warns them that multiple factions are now probably tracking them
* asks them to keep watching for the hidden move behind the public one
* offers future Harper contact

# Endcap complication

Use **one** of these to close the session.

## Option A — Theater servant is a watcher

The servant who kept looking toward the party slips away after intermission.

### Run as short skill challenge

3 successes before 2 failures.

Checks:

* **Perception DC 12**
* **Acrobatics DC 12**
* **Investigation DC 13**
* **Athletics DC 13**
* **Intimidation DC 14**

### On success

They catch the watcher and recover:

* a marked guest list
* a folded note
* a brass eye token
* instructions to observe “the old lord’s box”

### Dropped note handout

> Observe the box.
> Confirm the contacts.
> Do not engage.

This works very well if you want a crisp finish.

---

## Option B — Message from Trollskull

At intermission or just after, a runner finds them:

* upstairs window tampered with again
* nothing stolen
* someone came back

This is better if you want next session to start at Trollskull.

---

## Option C — The chandlery face is here

Someone tied to the afternoon’s clue follow-up spots the party and bolts backstage.

This ties the entire session into one clean thread.

### My recommendation

Use **Option C** if you want the strongest feeling of continuity.
Use **Option A** if you want the cleanest faction-intrigue ending.

* calm, observant, compassionate
* more interested in what kind of danger this is than in gossip

* practical, neighborly, slightly wary
* cares because danger in the alley affects everyone

* breezy on the surface
* always a little too well prepared
* notices more than he comments on

## Surviving Handler

* frightened, cornered, not loyal enough to die silently
* wants protection more than money
* knows fragments, not answers

## Mirt

* genial, sharp, dangerous underneath
* rewards thought, not bravado
* wants to know whether they can read the city, not just hit what jumps out
