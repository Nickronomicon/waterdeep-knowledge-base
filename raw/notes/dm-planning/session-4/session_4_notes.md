# NOTES

You should avoid running this as “Vajra scene, then Mirt scene, then maybe a random fight.” That will feel like two appointments plus filler.

The cleaner structure is:

1. **Blackstaff Tower = recruitment + actionable task**
2. **Same day = combat generated by that task**
3. **Opera next evening = payoff, new pressure, and a hard choice**

That matches the adventure’s rhythm better: Chapter 2 is supposed to interweave faction material with the life of Trollskull Alley, not replace the session with standalone briefings, and the Remix explicitly benefits from factions acting proactively against the PCs.  

### Recommended session spine

#### Scene 1: On the way to Blackstaff Tower

Start with momentum, not arrival.

As they cross Castle Ward, let them notice they are being watched:

* a ragged “beggar” who is too clean
* a rooftop silhouette that vanishes
* a child who repeats a phrase one PC said privately the day before

This is not combat yet. It is a pressure signal: the party’s notoriety in Trollskull Alley has moved beyond neighbors and guilds.

That sets up Vajra as someone reacting to a real situation, not just handing out a faction pamphlet.

---

### Scene 2: Blackstaff Tower audience

The book’s default setup is simple: Vajra summons them by *sending*, invites them to Blackstaff Tower, and offers Gray Hands membership as a test of loyalty to Waterdeep.  

#### How to run Vajra

Keep her scene short, sharp, and specific. She should not feel like a quest dispenser. She should feel like someone who already knows more than the PCs do.

#### Suggested beats

* Vajra confirms she knows they rescued Renaer and have drawn attention.
* She frames the city as being under pressure from multiple covert actors.
* She offers **Gray Hands**, not full Force Grey. The text is clear that Gray Hands is the entry point; Force Grey is the elite cadre above that. 
* She gives them a field test that can become immediate action.

#### Best mission to pivot into your actual campaign

Do **not** use Hlam as written unless you want a travel detour. It is fine on paper, but it pulls attention away from Waterdeep right when your campaign needs urban velocity. The printed Gray Hands missions include Hlam at 2nd level and Zelifarn at 3rd, but neither is ideal for your current pacing. 

Instead, have Vajra say something like:

> “I am less interested in whether you can swing a blade than whether you can keep your heads when the city turns strange around you. Something is moving through the Dock Ward at dusk. Xanathar likes to release monsters to spread panic and pull the Watch thin. Investigate. If you can end it cleanly, you’ve proven useful.”

This is anchored in existing faction material: the adventure explicitly uses Xanathar-released monsters as urban distractions, including a grell example. 

#### Add one strong roleplay detail

Use Vajra to clock something personal in the party:

* she recognizes noble etiquette in Jonathan
* she studies Davy too closely, as if she almost sees through the disguise
* she addresses Elior’s scar reaction without fully explaining it
* she gives Broethuul the kind of measured respect he likely doesn’t get elsewhere

That makes the summons matter.

---

### Scene 3: Immediate combat — Dock Ward monster hunt

This should be your combat.

#### Why this works

* It comes directly out of Vajra’s summons.
* It is urban and public, so it changes how the party is seen.
* It reinforces that faction membership means consequences, not just invitations.
* It avoids stalling the Grand Game.

#### Use

A **grell** if the party is sturdy and level 2-3 with 4+ PCs.
If not, downgrade to:

* 1 gazer + 3 kenku toughs
* 2 carrion crawlers in alleys/sewers
* 1 intellect devourer handler with 2 thugs
* a wounded grell with reduced hp and no surprise grab opening

The published Emerald Enclave mission says Xanathar releases monsters to distract the authorities, specifically mentioning a grell in the Dock Ward. That gives you solid source support for the premise. 

#### Run it as a set piece, not a street brawl

The monster is not standing in an empty map square waiting to roll initiative.

Use a **3-stage fight**:

1. **Panic:** screaming civilians, overturned fish carts, slippery cobbles, maybe a hanging sign falls
2. **Verticality:** rooftops, balconies, cargo cranes, sewer grate
3. **Choice:** save civilians or secure the kill

That lets the combat say something about the party.

#### Reward

When they report back, Vajra doesn’t shower them with loot. She gives:

* Gray Hands tokens or writs
* legal cover if trouble finds them
* one actionable tip about the city’s covert war
* maybe one loaned uncommon item for one mission, which fits the Force Grey support model in *Introduction*. 

---

### Scene 4: Fallout at Trollskull Manor

Give them 20-30 minutes of table time back at Trollskull.

This is where you cash in the alley cast:

* Urchins are thrilled and dramatize the fight inaccurately
* Fala or Tally congratulates them
* Frewn pretends concern while fishing for details
* Lif silently sets out drinks for the “real heroes of the ward”

This keeps Trollskull relevant instead of making the whole campaign drift to Castle Ward institutions. Trollskull Alley is meant to stay central to the party’s day-to-day life. 

You can also drop one clue that someone was searching near the manor while they were away.

---

### Scene 5: The opera, next evening

The Harpers’ invitation is straightforward: tickets to *The Fall of Tiamat* at the Lightsinger Theater, meet Mirt in his private box at intermission, formal attire required. 

This scene should be elegant, but not passive.

#### What Mirt should do

Mirt should not just say, “Join the Harpers.” He should:

* congratulate them on drawing the attention of more dangerous people than they realize
* pitch the Harpers as the faction that understands secrets, not force
* ask what they think Waterdeep needs most: order, truth, justice, profit, or survival

He is testing temperament, not competence.

#### Make the opera useful

During the performance or intermission:

* Renaer is present and can bridge Vajra/Mirt if needed
* the Cassalanters are present if you want to foreshadow them
* Jalester is present if you want to triangulate law, espionage, and civic power
* a disguised drow observer or Harper counterspy notices the party

This is consistent with the social density the Remix leans into for city events. 

---

### Optional second conflict at the opera

I would not make this a full second combat unless your table wants a high-action session. One real combat is enough.

Instead, use a **contained disturbance**:

* a stage rigging “accident” that is actually sabotage
* a pickpocket stealing from Mirt’s box
* a spy trying to slip out after overhearing too much
* a noble publicly recognizing one of the PCs from the Crommor scandal fallout

That gives tension without turning the theater into another dungeon room.

If you do want a second action beat, make it a **chase** out of the theater, not another slugfest.

---

### Best version of the whole session

Here is the version I would run.

#### Act 1 — The summons

* ominous tail on the way to Blackstaff Tower
* Vajra scene: brief, incisive, offers Gray Hands probation
* mission: stop a Xanathar-linked urban threat tonight

#### Act 2 — The proof

* investigation in Dock Ward
* one substantial combat with public stakes
* survivors/witnesses create reputation consequences

#### Act 3 — The city reacts

* return to Trollskull
* short decompression with neighbors and faction feelers
* one sign they are now on multiple boards at once

#### Act 4 — The opera

* formalwear, class contrast, Mirt’s invitation pays off
* intermission box conversation
* subtle complication or chase, not a full battle
* end with a new lead and at least one new enemy or patron

Make Mirt the **interpretation** of action.

One gives them the city’s hard edge. The other shows them its hidden one.

# SESSION OUTLINE

Yes. As written, that pacing is compressed in the wrong place.

If it is already **sunset** when they head to Blackstaff Tower, you should not force “Vajra briefing → Dock Ward investigation → major combat → Trollskull fallout” all into the same night unless you want the session to feel rushed and implausibly busy.

The cleaner fix is to treat Vajra’s summons as a **night briefing that sets up a next-day operation**.

### Revised session spine

#### Tonight

1. Road to Blackstaff Tower
2. Vajra audience
3. Short nighttime follow-up scene
4. Return to Trollskull / late-night intrusion or omen

#### Tomorrow

5. Morning investigation
6. Afternoon combat
7. Brief reset
8. Opera with Mirt that evening

That uses the 20–24 hour window instead of fighting it.

## Revised DM-facing structure

### TONIGHT, SCENE 1: On the way to Blackstaff Tower

Keep this as written:

* party notices they are being watched
* small clue, no combat
* Blackstaff Tower interrupts escalation

This works even better at sunset, because Castle Ward is shifting from respectable evening traffic into lamplit shadows.

#### Boxed read-aloud

> The last gold of evening is draining from the high stone of Castle Ward, and lamplighters are beginning their rounds. Shadows stretch long across the street.
>
> At first it feels like ordinary city motion. Then the pattern sharpens. A cloaked figure pauses whenever you pause. A raven watches from a chimney and then from another. A child at a corner says, to no one you can see, “They’re almost there,” and hurries off into the dusk.

---

### TONIGHT, SCENE 2: Vajra’s summons becomes a briefing, not a mission launch

This is the key fix.

Vajra should not send them out to immediately solve a full crisis in Dock Ward at night unless you want to skip sleep, planning, and social transition. Instead, she gives them:

* a **problem**
* a **deadline**
* a **reason it can wait until morning**
* maybe **one quick check they can do tonight** if they are restless

#### Revised Vajra framing

> “This is not a matter for blunt panic. Something was loosed in the Dock Ward last night and may move again tomorrow. I want it investigated before the city wakes fully to the pattern.”

That makes tomorrow’s operation feel urgent without requiring immediate action.

#### What Vajra asks for

She gives them a **Gray Hands probationary assignment**:

* investigate a Dock Ward disturbance
* determine whether Xanathar or another faction is behind it
* stop it if it is still active
* report back before nightfall if possible

#### What she specifically says about timing

> “You may go now, if you enjoy chasing rumors in the dark. I recommend you go at first light. Witnesses talk more freely in daylight, and Dock Ward lies more cleanly when the taverns empty.”

That line solves your timing issue in-world.

#### Optional reward / support

Because they are going tomorrow, Vajra can plausibly help:

* a written token marking them as acting for the Blackstaff
* one potion of healing for the group
* a name to ask for in Dock Ward
* a warning that the Watch may already be compromised or at least unhelpful

---

### TONIGHT, SCENE 3: Give them a short, non-main-combat follow-up

You still need something to happen tonight, or the session risks becoming “walk, briefing, sleep.”

Do **not** make it the main warehouse fight. Make it a **probe**.

Choose one:

#### Option A: Street ambush lite

On the way back from Blackstaff Tower, two low-rent toughs try to size them up or shake them down, clearly under orders to test them.

Use:

* 2 thugs or 3 weak ruffians
* quick 2–3 round scuffle or interrogation
* one carries an eye token or nonsense instructions

This is not the session’s showcase combat. It is a pressure signal.

#### Option B: Trollskull intrusion

When they return home, someone has already been inside or is **currently** inside.

Best version:

* upper window open
* faint movement upstairs
* a single spy or burglar bolts across rooftops if discovered

This gives you tension without consuming the next day’s main set piece.

#### Option C: Urchin warning

Nat/Jenks/Squiddly intercepted or saw something:

* “A weird little floaty eye was outside your place.”
* “A man with a black glove asked whether you were home.”
* “Somebody tried your upstairs window.”

This is lowest action, highest setup.

---

### TONIGHT, SCENE 4: End the night with a hook, not exhaustion

By bedtime, the party should know:

* Vajra has marked them as useful
* someone else is watching them
* tomorrow’s Dock Ward job matters
* they still have the opera tomorrow night

That creates anticipation instead of overload.

---

## TOMORROW MORNING: Investigation phase

Now the Dock Ward sequence has room to breathe.

### SCENE 5: Morning legwork

This is where Ma Sella, Harl Brassk, the bent grate, the roof trail, and dock rumors come in. In daylight, all of those clues make more sense and are easier to stage.

#### Why this is better than same-night

* witnesses are awake and available
* the warehouse can be scouted before the fight
* you can build tension toward the afternoon combat
* it leaves room for the opera without absurd pacing

#### Boxed read-aloud

> Morning in the Dock Ward smells of brine, fish guts, old timber, and wet rope. Men shout over cargo, gulls scream overhead, and every third person swears they saw something terrible last night. By the look of them, at least half are lying. The other half might be worse.

#### Structure

Run this as a short node investigation:

* Ma Sella saw the thing
* bent sewer grate
* rooftop/slime trail
* someone mentions “crate delivery before dawn”
* maybe a dock thug tries to obstruct them

---

## TOMORROW EARLY AFTERNOON: Main combat

### SCENE 6: Warehouse set-piece fight

This becomes the session’s main combat, and it lands much better in daylight or gray afternoon than jammed into midnight.

#### Why afternoon works

It gives them time to investigate first and lets the fight interrupt their conclusions:

* they identify the warehouse
* they think they are about to search it
* then the threat triggers, escapes, feeds, or breaks loose

#### Best trigger

As they approach or scout:

* scream from inside
* crate bursts open
* grell drops from rafters
* handlers panic

That preserves the surprise.

#### Main encounter

Still use:

* 1 grell
* 2 Xanathar thugs
* 1 civilian or trapped worker

Or adjust for party level.

#### Revised boxed read-aloud

> The warehouse doors stand half-open in the flat light of afternoon. Inside, stacked crates and hanging hooks throw long bars of shadow across the floorboards. The place should be loud with work, but instead it is wrongfully quiet.
>
> Then something slams into the far crate stack from above. A worker screams. Splinters spray outward. And from the dim rafters a pulsing, tentacled horror unfolds itself into the air.

---

## TOMORROW LATE AFTERNOON: Reset window

### SCENE 7: Short reset at Trollskull

After the fight, give them a compact decompression scene:

* neighbors react
* urchins dramatize
* Frewn fishes for information
* Lif silently serves drinks

This needs to be brief now, because the opera is tonight.

#### Important addition

This is the ideal place to introduce:

* “formalwear problem”
* cleanup / blood / time pressure
* a social beat before the theater

That contrast is useful:
they go from fish-blood and warehouse dust to high society in only a few hours.

#### Nice practical beat

One neighbor or faction contact helps them get presentable enough for the opera.

Examples:

* Fala fixes stains with druidcraft and common sense
* Tally loans a decent cloak
* Jonathan actually owns proper formalwear and becomes the group’s emergency court tutor
* Threestrings arranged for this contingency because he is not stupid

---

## TOMORROW EVENING: Opera and Mirt

### SCENE 8: Lightsinger Theater

Now the opera becomes payoff for a full day, not a third act stapled onto an already overstuffed night.

The invitation itself is still correct: the Harpers send tickets and direct the PCs to meet Mirt at intermission in a private box. 

#### Why this now works

They arrive:

* tired
* dirty underneath clean clothes
* already aware they are being watched
* carrying fresh evidence from a Gray Hands mission

That gives Mirt something real to discuss.

---

### SCENE 9: Mirt’s intermission pitch

This now works better because the party has actually done something for Waterdeep in the last 12 hours.

Mirt can react to fresh news:

> “I heard there was a commotion by the harbor. Curious how quickly the city produces useful people when embarrassed.”

He offers the Harper angle:

* Vajra uses force and authority
* Harpers use discretion and information
* both are now interested in the party, which is flattering and dangerous

---

### SCENE 10: Optional opera complication

Keep this small:

* overheard spy
* backstage pursuit
* suspicious note
* marked guest list

No second full battle.
