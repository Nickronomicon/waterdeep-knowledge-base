# DM Prep Sheet: Blackstaff’s Briefing, Dock Ward Hunt, and Mirt at the Opera

This revised version fixes the timing issue: it assumes the party is already on the way to Blackstaff Tower at **sunset**, with the rest of this evening and all of tomorrow before meeting Mirt at the opera tomorrow night. It keeps the published faction hooks intact: Vajra summons promising adventurers to Blackstaff Tower for Gray Hands work, and the Harpers invite them to meet Mirt at intermission at the Lightsinger Theater.   It also stays aligned with the adventure’s expectation that Trollskull Alley remains central to the party’s life between larger intrigue beats. 

---

# Session Summary

At sunset, the party heads to Blackstaff Tower and realizes they are being watched. Vajra Safahr offers them probationary Gray Hands work and briefs them on a suspicious Dock Ward incident, but explicitly advises them to investigate at first light rather than chase rumors through the night. On the way home, the party experiences a short pressure beat: a probing ambush, an intrusion at Trollskull, or an urchin warning. The next morning, they investigate the Dock Ward disturbance. By early afternoon, that investigation leads to a warehouse set-piece combat against a Xanathar-linked threat. After a brief reset at Trollskull Manor, the party attends the opera, meets Mirt at intermission, and ends the evening with a spy complication or suspicious note.

---

# TONIGHT

## 1. Scene: Watched on the Way to Blackstaff Tower

### Purpose

Establish that the party is now visible to other factions and no longer moving anonymously through Waterdeep.

### Boxed Read-Aloud

> The last gold of evening is draining from the high stone of Castle Ward, and lamplighters are beginning their rounds. Shadows stretch long across the street.
>
> At first it feels like ordinary city motion. Then the pattern sharpens. A cloaked figure pauses whenever you pause. A raven watches from a chimney and then from another. A child at a corner says, to no one you can see, “They’re almost there,” and hurries off into the dusk.

### What Is Happening

Someone is observing the party. Leave the identity unresolved:

* Xanathar watcher
* Harper counterspy
* Bregan D’Aerthe scout
* Blackstaff agent confirming their route

### Checks

* **Wisdom (Perception) DC 13:** Notice repeated faces or vantage points
* **Wisdom (Insight) DC 14:** This is coordinated observation
* **Intelligence (Investigation) DC 15:** A chalk mark or dropped token is likely part of a tracking/signaling system

### If the party pursues

Do not let this become a full chase. The watcher escapes into side streets or rooftops, leaving behind:

* cheap brass eye token
* black thread snagged on brick
* chalk mark by a drain
* folded scrap with a meaningless meeting time

### GM Note

Blackstaff Tower should interrupt escalation. The point is unease, not resolution.

### Support Vajra Can Offer

## 5. Scene: Morning Investigation in Dock Ward

The adventure already supports faction-related urban incidents and city-centered investigations between Trollskull Alley and larger villain material.  

### Purpose

Create a clean, daylight investigation that leads naturally into combat.

### Boxed Read-Aloud

> Morning in the Dock Ward smells of brine, fish guts, old timber, and wet rope. Men shout over cargo, gulls scream overhead, and every third person swears they saw something terrible last night. By the look of them, at least half are lying. The other half might be worse.

### Core Clues

The party only needs two of the following three.

---

### Clue A: Ma Sella

A fish seller and the best eyewitness because she actually paid attention.

#### Boxed Read-Aloud

> The old fishwife is built like a bollard and smells like she’s been winning arguments over mackerel for forty years. She squints at you, wipes one wet hand on her apron, and says, “You’re not Watch. Good. Watch asks stupid questions.”

#### What she saw

* “Something ugly dropped off a warehouse roof.”
* “Tentacles. Beak. Floating. Wrong.”
* “Two alley-rats were shouting at it like it was supposed to listen.”

#### Checks

* **Charisma (Persuasion) DC 11:** She shares everything useful
* **Wisdom (Insight) DC 13:** She is reliable on the important details

---

### Clue B: Rooftop Trail

On or near the warehouse roof:

* blood drag marks
* dropped boot
* slime smear
* broken skylight

#### Checks

* **Wisdom (Survival) DC 12:** Creature or victim moved through the skylight
* **Intelligence (Investigation) DC 13:** At least two humans were here too

---

### Clue C: Bent Sewer Grate

Nearby alley:

* grate bent upward
* muddy boot prints
* gouges on stone

#### Checks

* **Intelligence (Investigation) DC 14:** Used as an escape route

## 6. Scene: Early Afternoon Warehouse Combat

The published faction material explicitly includes urban monster deployment and Xanathar-linked creature threats in the city.  This fight uses that logic, but puts it in a stronger timeline slot.

### Purpose

Main combat of the session.

### Trigger

The party locates the correct warehouse and begins to scout it. Just as they are about to enter or conclude it is empty, the threat erupts.

### Boxed Read-Aloud

> The warehouse doors stand half-open in the flat light of afternoon. Inside, stacked crates and hanging hooks throw long bars of shadow across the floorboards. The place should be loud with work, but instead it is wrongfully quiet.
>
> Then something slams into the far crate stack from above. A worker screams. Splinters spray outward. And from the dim rafters a pulsing, tentacled horror unfolds itself into the air.

### Recommended Encounter

For a level 2–3 party of 4–5:

* 1 **Grell**
* 2 **Xanathar Thugs**
* 1 trapped worker or civilian

### Encounter Logic

The thugs were transporting, baiting, or loosing the creature to create chaos. It is not fully under their control.

---

## Quick Encounter Stats

### Grell

* AC 12
* HP 55
* Fly 30 ft, hover
* Tentacles: +4 to hit, reach 10 ft, grapple/paralysis rider
* Beak vs grappled target
* Tactics:

  * starts above the battlefield
  * attacks vulnerable or isolated target
  * retreats if reduced below 15 HP and has a path out

### Xanathar Thugs (2)

* AC 11
* HP 32 each
* Mace: +4 to hit, 1d6+2
* Heavy crossbow: +2 to hit, 1d10
* Tactics:

  * one tries to recover evidence or escape
  * one attempts to redirect the grell, fails, then panics
  * flee if one dies and the monster is no longer useful

### Trapped Worker

* AC 10
* HP 4
* prone, frightened
* can become witness if saved

## 9. Scene: Mirt at Intermission

### Purpose

Offer a second factional lens on Waterdeep: subtlety instead of authority.

### Boxed Read-Aloud

> The first act ends in thunderous applause. Servants move like clockwork with wine and folded napkins. In the adjoining private box lounges a man who looks equal parts indulgent grandfather, retired predator, and delighted conspirator.
>
> Mirt beams at you over the rim of his glass. “My dear friends,” he says, “Waterdeep is never more honest than when everyone in the room is pretending.”

### What Mirt Wants

He is not recruiting muscle. He is testing judgment.

### Talking Points

* Renaer spoke well of them
* the city is under strain from more than ordinary crime
* panic in the Dock Ward was useful to someone
* Harpers value truth, discretion, and independent minds

### Questions Mirt Asks

* “Who benefits from public chaos?”
* “What kind of fool looses a monster in the open unless the confusion itself is the point?”
* “What do you think matters more in Waterdeep: who is powerful, or who knows when to be absent?”

### If the party impresses him

* Harper contact phrase
* future invitation
* small favor, purse, or information promise

## 10. Scene: Spy Complication

Do not run a second full combat. Make this a short pursuit or interception.

### Trigger

One PC notices a figure slipping away from a service corridor during or after intermission.

### Boxed Read-Aloud

> As the crowd shifts for intermission, one movement cuts against the rhythm of servants and guests: a figure in plain livery, head down, moving too quickly and too carefully toward the backstage passage.
>
> Just before they disappear, they glance back once. Not at Mirt. At you.

### Spy Options

Choose one:

* disguised drow courier
* Xanathar agent in servant clothes
* noble house spy with marked guest list

### Run as Simple Skill Challenge

First to 3 successes before 2 failures.

Possible checks:

* **Dexterity (Acrobatics) DC 12:** over scenery and rigging
* **Strength (Athletics) DC 13:** through blocked passage
* **Wisdom (Perception) DC 12:** keep sight through crowds
* **Intelligence (Investigation) DC 13:** predict exit
* **Charisma (Intimidation) DC 14:** freeze them with command

### If the spy is caught

Recover one:

* guest list with marked names
* note: “Observe the old lord’s box. Confirm contacts.”
* black glove with hidden pocket
* eye token or coded scrap

### If the spy escapes

They leave behind:

* torn note
* glove
* ribbon
* distinctive scent or seal

### Handout C: Dropped Note

> Enjoy the tragedy.
> You are sitting in the wrong part of the story.
