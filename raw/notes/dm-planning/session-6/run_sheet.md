# Session 6

## Approaching Lightsinger Theater

> Sunset spills copper and rose across the Castle Ward as you approach the Lightsinger Theater, its white stone facade glowing like a lantern against the deepening blue of evening. Carriages rattle up in a steady line, wheels hissing over damp cobbles while liveried footmen open doors for jeweled nobles and masked socialites. Gas lamps flicker to life along the avenue, catching on gilt trim, polished brass, and the theater's towering stained-glass oculus where a celestial choir is frozen in color.
>
> Hawkers drift along the edge of the crowd with sugared nuts and hot wine, but their voices stay low here; this is old-money ground, and even the street noise seems to know it. Above the broad front steps, banners of midnight blue and gold sway in the harbor wind. Music leaks faintly from within - strings tuning, a horn calling once, then silence - followed by the collective murmur of Waterdeep's elite assembling to be seen.

## Meeting Mirt

> Private Box C smells of good brandy and old money.
>
> A broad, aging man lounges in a chair that looks as though it has had to surrender to him by force of personality. His clothes are sumptuous without being tasteful, his rings are heavy, and his expression suggests that he has spent a lifetime collecting secrets and deciding which of them are worth the bother.
>
> He does not rise.
>
> Instead, he lifts his glass slightly in greeting and says:
>
> “So. These are Renaer’s good bets.”
>
> His gaze travels across the party once, lazily.
>
> It is the laziest thing anyone in the room has done all evening.
>
> And somehow the most thorough.

## The Chase

First to 3 successes before 2 failures.

> The suspect bursts into the night and vanishes into the web of Castle Ward side streets, where opera light gives way to shuttered windows, narrow alleys, and long pools of lamplight broken by deep shadow. Behind you, the theater doors spill outraged voices and confused servants into the street.
>
> Ahead: pounding boots, a turning silhouette, then darkness.

Possible checks:

* **Dexterity (Acrobatics) DC 13:** vault rain barrels, low fences, and stacked crates without losing speed.
* **Strength (Athletics) DC 14:** shoulder through a bolted service gate or shove past a wagon blocking a lane.
* **Wisdom (Perception) DC 13:** track movement by sound and fleeting shadow when the target slips out of sight.
* **Intelligence (Investigation) DC 14:** read alley layouts and predict the route toward a likely escape street.
* **Charisma (Intimidation) DC 15:** bark a command at loiterers or teamsters to force them to point out where the runner went.
* **Dexterity (Stealth) DC 13:** cut through a parallel lane quietly to intercept without announcing your approach.

Complications (trigger after each failure or whenever pacing needs pressure):

* A fishmonger cart topples into the lane. **Dexterity saving throw DC 12** or lose momentum.
* A City Watch lantern patrol turns the corner and mistakes the party for troublemakers. **Charisma (Persuasion) DC 13** to keep moving without being delayed.
* The fugitive douses a lamp with a thrown cloak, plunging the alley into darkness. Next tracking check is made at disadvantage unless someone creates light.

If success:
- the man refuses to speak, just glares with hostility.
- recover from his person a note: “Observe the old lord’s box. Confirm contacts.”
- The party is ambushed (without the spy as a combatant) on the way home

If failure:
- The party catches up to the spy speaking to another man in the street.
- This turns into an ambush (they spy is an extra combatant)
