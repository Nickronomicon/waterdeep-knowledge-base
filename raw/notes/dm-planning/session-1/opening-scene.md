The taproom of the Yawning Portal is loud even by Dock Ward standards.

You sit around a sturdy wooden table lit by a bright, guttering candle. Plates cleared of quipper and chips sit pushed aside, tankards of Shadowdark ale half-drained. Gamblers shout over dice. A trio of adventurers belt out something obscene about a dragon and a barmaid. Three tables over, a young bard strums bravely — and badly.

Standing atop a chair near the hearth, Volothamp Geddarm finishes his tale with theatrical grandeur.

> “—and thus concluded the glorious exploits of the famiglia di Noci! A saga of loyalty, daring, and only minimal arson!”

Scattered applause. Someone boos. Someone else yells, “They still owe me for that wagon!”

Volo bows deeply.

> “And should any of you desire your own names immortalized in print, do come speak with me—”

A shout cuts him off.

> “Ya pig! Like killin’ me mates, does ya?!”

The music stops.

Chairs scrape.

A seven-foot-tall half-orc woman rises from a nearby table, broad-shouldered and dangerous. Before she can fully turn, a wild punch swings in from a shaved-headed human whose scalp is covered in eye-shaped tattoos. Four rough-looking men crowd behind him, hands already curling into fists.

The half-orc slowly cracks her knuckles.

She roars and launches herself at him.

The two crash into a table, sending tankards flying. Spectators surge inward instantly, forming a tight ring of shouting encouragement and wagers.

You catch glimpses between shoulders and elbows — the tattooed man, blood already running from his lip; the half-orc driving him backward; the other four thugs closing in.

What do you do?

Before the fight can resolve—

A new sound cuts through the shouting.

A wet scraping. Stone against claw.

Then screams.

All heads turn toward the massive well at the center of the tavern.

A hulking shape hauls itself up from the darkness — warty green skin, a tangled nest of wiry black hair, long carrot-shaped nose, bloodshot eyes blazing with hunger.

As it bares yellow teeth and howls, you see bat-like creatures clinging to its flesh, bloated and twitching. Three more circle above it like flies over carrion.

For one heartbeat, the entire tavern freezes.

The barkeep does not.

Durnan reaches beneath the bar and draws a massive greatsword in one smooth motion. He bellows,

> “Troll!”

Panic erupts.

Patrons dive for cover. Gamblers scatter. The bard vanishes under a table.

The bloated stirges detach and flutter lazily back down the shaft — but three peel away and dive toward the nearest warm bodies.

The troll drops heavily onto the taproom floor, rising to its full, horrifying height.

Durnan vaults the bar and charges, blade flashing in the lanternlight.

As steel meets regenerating flesh, he shouts over his shoulder:
> “Take the stirges! When it falls, oil and fire!”

If she’s still conscious, Yagra wrenches free of the brawl and turns on the monster with a snarl. The Xanathar thugs hesitate only a moment before deciding this is not their problem.

The troll roars.

The stirges dive.
