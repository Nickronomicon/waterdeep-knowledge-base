## Trollskull Grand Opening
* Hiring decisions: Decide how many staff they want/need. Use the list of Bartenders/Barmaids/Cooks provided in the Alexandrian Remix (Addendum: A Night in Trollskull Manor) -> Limit to 20 minutes or so
* Task the players with "planning" their grand opening, i.e. have them hand out roles for each player/npc during the opening. Have them come up with how they will apply their skillset to make the grand opening a success or not. Use this to for skill check rolls during the opening itself. -> Limit to 15 minutes MAX
    * Also ask if they want to send invitations and to whom. Basically ignore their invitation list and stick with a pre-planned guest list, just make up excuses for anyone invited that did not appear.
* After planning, introduce a story beat - either Faction related, backstory related, or foreshadowing for the larger campaign - TBD -> 15-20 minutes
* The grand opening, needs:
    - Guest list -> who makes an appearance and when, suggested to divide the night into 2 phases, decide who is present for first, second, or both.
    - Series of minor events -> maybe a barfight, maybe some Frewn interference, maybe just the little things that can go wrong at a large party. Require skill checks to resolve them
    - Conversational snippets between guests to help with anchoring existing story beats or foreshadowing future ones
* End with some sort of encounter? These players like and expect combat. Preferably combat is post Grand Opening and slightly removed from the premises of Trollskull Manor
    * Optionally, this encounter could happen between phase 1 and phase 2 of the grand opening, but this runs the risk of pushing phase 2 off to the next session (combat takes a long time with this group)

## Rolling for Results
* Keep a tally of success(+1)/fail(-1)/crit success(+2)/crit fail(-2) during the grand opening. These will feed into a modifier for their "grand opening performance" rolls

* Hiring both Miri and Corvin adds a +3 to the result roll every 10-day.
* Lif adds +2 to the result roll
* Hiring other staff will also add to the result roll, but I'll decide the modifier after they negotiate the wage. Target staff wage level: <= 10 gp
* Maintenance/Materials/Upkeep: 40 gp/10-day
* Guild dues + fees: 10 gp/10-day
* Staff wages: <= 10 gp/10-day (currently 2gp)
* Cassalanter loan: 40 gp/10-day (60 gp after four months, current principal: 2000 gp)

* **Total: 92-100 gp/10-day**


> **Running a Business (Taken from DM's Guide, Chapter 6)**
> |d100 + Days|Result|
> |---|---|
> |01–20|You must pay one and a half times the business’s maintenance cost for each of the days.|
> |21–30|You must pay the business’s full maintenance cost for each of the days.|
> |31–40|You must pay half the business’s maintenance cost for each of the days. Profits cover the other half.|
> |41–60|The business covers its own maintenance cost for each of the days. It earns (or loses) a profit of 2d4 - 5 gp.|
> |61–80|The business covers its own maintenance cost for each of the days. It earns a profit of 1d6 × 5 gp.|
> |81–90|The business covers its own maintenance cost for each of the days. It earns a profit of 2d8 × 5 gp.|
> |91 or higher|The business covers its own maintenance cost for each of the days. It earns a profit of 3d10 × 5 gp.|

### Grand Opening Result Roll
After each phase, roll the above table with associated modifiers (staff + skill checks). After the grand opening, present them with what remains of their loan: (phase 1 roll) + (phase 2 roll) + 1d6 + 500gp.
> Suggest having the "star" of each phase (whoever had the most or most memorable skill checks) make the d100 roll, but don't tell them what the table says.

Range of results: [200 gp .. 600 gp]

### Every 10-Day Result Roll

If a player spends money promoting the business (beyond operating costs) add +1 to the result roll for each 1 gp spent.

If players come up short and can't afford to pay the total, they can choose who to stiff:

|Entity|Modifier||
|---|---|---|
|Staff|+0 (no staff bonus)|If this is the second (consecutive) or third (non-consecutive) time, the staff memeber may leave. Back wages are still owed.
|Guilds|-1 for each gp owed|Guilds may start to exert other pressure for non-payment. Unpaid dues are still owed|
|Maintenance / Material / Upkeep|-1 for each gp unpaid|Unpaid operating expenses are not owed the next 10-day|
|Cassalanter|no modifier|**First offense**: not-so-subtle threats, **Subsequent**: Drastic action taken against players|

**Frewn Sabotage**: Depending on what and how successful, it will subtract from the result roll. This should be communicated to the players like, 
> "Due to the enormous fine for rat droppings, you still owe 30gp after all expenses are paid,"

or

> "Despite the damage to your cellar, you still made 15 gp this 10-day."

The fully staffed bar should add approx +8 or so to the result roll, which means that each 10-day the players will roll 1d100+18

Range of results: [-150 gp .. 150 gp]

Assuming all roll probabilities are equal, then over time this should result in an average profit range of [-12 gp 4 sp .. 38 gp 6 sp] (depending on profit rolls)

Again, assuming all profit roll probabilites are equal, the average profit on a long running timescale (with no modifications to loan or staffing) should converge around [13 gp 1 sp] per 10-day.
